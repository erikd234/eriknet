Back to being a dumb tourist.

When we got to the camp site last night at 9:30 pm we thought only things about 3 things.

Dinner, ocean, sleep.

So we did not pay any attention to where we set up our tents or anything else we did.

When we woke up in the morning the Greek owner of the pool bar we had camped beside was upset we had camped near his bar - fortunately they are not open yet and today was their first day setting things up for the season. We said sorry and he forgave us - but a good lesson for us to be more careful where we set up our tents at night ğŸ˜….

Last nights campsite

After yesterday we were very tired and decided to just take a slow easy day on the bike. We slowly drifted down the coast on our bikes - stopping under some trees in the shade to dodge the afternoon heat.

Biking along the coast

On the beach I just practiced my Greek and Italian, went for a swim and sat in the shade. We were so tired from yesterday and my leg was still sore from the fall the day before so we thought about going beach to beach all day biking only small amounts in the evening when the temperature cooled down.

Sitting on the beach for hours

Eventually at around 4pm, we decided to keep biking. I got on my bike and started to pedal and for the first 10 metres I felt like i was biking really slow. I quickly realized that my rear tire was flat. We stopped and looked at the tire and found that the tire was not punctured but rather the entire side of the outside tire ripped open. My rear tire had completely failed, there was no tread left to grip the road and the sides were splitting.

This must have been from the heavy weight on the rear of the bike (the bike with gear is about 50-60kg and I weight 94kg), and the fact that the tires were old that it was word down quickly.

The lack of tread on the tire could have also accounted for me slipping out on the curve the day before - i learned that it is very important to always check tire tread. I should have bought new tires before the trip

Fortunately the rear tire could hold air so I could keep riding slowly. I do not carry extra tires in my bags, just inner tubes (what actually holds the air in most bike tires) but there was no point in changing the tube since it would be exposed with a high risk of being punctured again because the outer protective layer of the tire was splitting in half.

I had to stop ever 20 mins to refill the air in my leaky tire.

biking on the highway - not too bad
