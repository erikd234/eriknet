#13 A German Surprise.

June 1st

Writing this during a rest day in Leonidio, we will talk about that tomorrow.

I am taking an easy today and I’ll catch up on the last few days, currently sitting in the sun on the beach ☺️.

I’ve been reading Shoe Dog by the founder of Nike. I got this quote from the book, that he got from some philosopher guy.

I’m too lazy on this beach chair to look up the quote again haha. I’ll paraphrase

“The person who moves mountains first starts by carrying away the small stones”

Anyway I liked it a lot so I thought I would share it. We have not biked that far yet, maybe 500-600 kilometres. But I feel as we have seen so much already. I’m excited for the coming weeks. Little by little we are making some progress.

okay, on with June 1st…

The night before June 1st we camped at a campsite right underneath the Acropolis in Mikinis.

We woke up in the morning at the campsite. The place was owned and run by an elderly Greek couple and they made us tea in the morning which was very nice. We then left the campsite.

We headed up the hill to another amazing ruins. I believe it was called the Ancient Mycenae Palace. I don’t know much about the history of this place but it was a Bronze Age castle. It was a very very old. When Socrates was alive in Greece it was already 1000 years old and ruins.

View from the top of the palace

We saw all the rocks there and they were… well rocks. I enjoyed the view from the top of the hill and the walking around the castle but I think I lacked the imagination to think of how this place looked a few thousand years before.

Ancient rocks

Oh yes, this ruin also had Europes oldest relief! A relief is a carving into a rock? Not sure, i briefly heard someone say what when we I asked what a relief was.

The oldest relief in Europe

We had biked here to see it, adding an extra day of biking and I was unsure if it was worth the visit, UNTIL we saw the tomb of some really important guy (I am terrible with these Greek names)

We approached the door and heard weird chanting songs coming from within the door.

The special tomb

When we entered the tomb we found a German school choir singing. It seems like they were just having fun singing and we got a free private show in the tomb for 20 mins. This was by far my favourite part of visiting the ruins.

Germans singing

The singing sounded wonderful inside the tomb. The reverberations echoed from the singing making beautiful harmonies. Perfect! I could not understand a word of the German songs but all three of us clapped loudly between songs.

I left that tomb feeling very alive.

We left the ruins, making some friends as we ate lunch. We had many people give us recomendaciones of places to visit in other parts of Europe.

We then set out on bike heading down a wonderful coastal road. This is one of the most beautiful coastal highways I have every biked on, we road on it for the last two days and I will talk more about it tomorrow.

Astros beach

After a day of riding we got to Astros, a small beach next to a lagoon. We set up our tents on the beach. Beach camping is amazing, free and quite. The only downside was the insane amounts of mosquitoes. In the 10 minutes I was setting up my tent. I got bitten maybe 10 times by mosquitoes - they would even follow me into the ocean. After I set up my tent I went inside and didn’t dare leave for any reason until the morning.

Nights accommodations.

Until tomorrow,
