#10 - Protected by a church

May 29th

Hey yall,

Writing this one on the road. Right now we are in the middle of a mountain, biking on a gravel service road, dodging the afternoon sun under a small tree.

yesterdays ride - screenshot from Adams Strava.

Yesterday, we woke up on the beach in Poros. The birds were defining. They were actually so loud I had trouble sleeping. I think it had to do with the rocks around, creating an eco.

We biked around the sea walls of Poros and saw massive boats. Many people come to Poros for boating, lots of chartered catamarans and sailboats. And this one massive 80 foot catamaran from Italy.

80 foot catamaran on the left

We left Poros and biked along the coast stopping for lunch in the beach. Adam and I split up temporarily from Kai early in the ride since Kai wanted to bike some hiking trails.

Coastal roads

After we split up we biked through small towns, eventually reaching the ferry terminal to the Island of Hydra. Adam and I decided to forgo taking the ferry across to Hydra and continue on. We biked more along the coast till the next town, stocked up on food. Then went over a hilly gravel road to get to our camping spot for the night.

Really fun gravel hill

Hours later, just before sunset it started to rain. Adam found a little spot on google maps to camp for the night on a beach and we told Kai to meet us there.

We arrived and it was still raining, and what we thought was a beach of google maps was actually all giant rocks.

But by good luck there was an old abandoned church near by and we camped under the cover of the church. The archway of the church protected us from rain as we cooked our dinner.

And the views of the ocean were not that bad :)

Until tomorrow, time for me to continue biking.
