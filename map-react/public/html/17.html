#17 - The never ending climb

June 5th

Hey,

First things first I am getting hungry. Please mail me food if you can.

Yesterday ate 1 kilogram of peanuts and 1 kilogram of Greek yogurt 😂

Mountain day

We left Leonidio. We almost stayed another day to climb but changed out minds and wanted to get back on the bike.

We said our final goodbye to Kai and left for the mountains.

I bought an awesome bandana in town- now I look like a real professional bike tourist

bandana is key

Adam and I left Leonidio heading for a bike route that would take us about 90km and 2000 metres of elevation to the top of the famous moni 💰peninsula in the Peloponnese - where we heard all the houses look like castles.

It was going to be a long day with the longest climb either Adam or I had ever done - the ride only had one climb that was 44 kilometres long and about 1600m of climbing. Through a giant rocky valley and over some mountains.

Entering the valley

it was an incredible climb through a canon. It was really hard but very rewarding. The views kept getting better and better every 10 minutes.

On the way up - wow

We found a another cliff side Monastery with incredible views - this one had actual monks in it which gave us free honey candy.

We got cold water in the monastery and continued. Even though the climb was really painful - it was still way easier that the last mountain crossing on gravel and dirt roads - and the views always made it worth it.

a small section of the climb

I keep saying this but Greece is probably the best place for biking on roads - great views, friendly drivers, and not that many cars. 80% of the cars are RVs with Germans on vacation and they always give lots of encouragement, food and water. They also drive very carefully in the narrow roads.

After biking for around 4 hours we made it to the top - which happened to be a mountain village. We had great Greek salad and espresso and relaxed for a bit. My life is forever changed, I will be eating so much Greek salad for the rest of my life.

We started the decent down the mountain and Wowow. It was the longest climb I have ever done and also one of the longest decent. 35 kilometres down the mountain.

The first big downhill was insane - serpentine switchbacks for what felt like hours.

Mountain village

One section of the decent we noticed some boxes off to the left of the road, behind some trees.

Then we also noticed thousands of bees flying everywhere all around us. It was a bee farm.

Part of the decent was a bee farm which stretched on for 1 or 2 kilometres. We were going at 70km /hour down this mountain while the bees were flying towards us and bouncing off our chest and face. Each bee felt like a paintball. I would dodge and weave with my head and curl into a little ball to minimize the chance to get hit by bees - I was sure to keep my mouth closed (one bee bounced right off my lip).

Near the tail end of the decent we approached yet another obstacle. I was trailing behind a van and it started to slow down and I heard very loud noises.

There were 200 goats walking down the road. I stopped and stared for a while completely baffled. I have never seen a goat crossing before. There was a van from Austria beside us and we were all laughing in disbelief about the goats.

Plowing through goats

Adam caught up a minute later and we pushed through the goats thanks to Adams ability to whistle very loudly.

At the very end the sheep dog chased us down the mountain 😂

Other than those two moments, when my mouth was stuck closed from bees and stuck open in disbelief about goats - I was screaming in joy about the how fun the way down was.

Best hill ever.

We moved to the next town, Geraki to see a castle - it was a Greek holiday, so we could not visit.- the castle was also on a massive hill so I was secretly glad to have the excuse not to climb up it.

The castle was up there 😅

We continued the ride down, arriving at the next town and finally we found a beach to camp on. We made an epic dinner feast - Adam made Indian food I made pasta.

Cooking pasta with fresh tomato sauce

We had a guard dog all night protecting us

And finally, the nights accommodations

Ciao famiglia.
