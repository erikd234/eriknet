#48 - A city preserved by a volcano - Pompei.

July 6th

Leaving my bike with my parking friends

July 6th

I woke up at the campsite. I slept in! It had been the first time in a long while that I was somewhere where I had no rush in the morning. My plan for the day was to visit Pompei, then go right to Napoli, which was a short 40km flat ride. So I just needed the afternoon.

I really thought hard about going over the top of the volcano to see the giant hole at the top of the volcano. I thought so hard about it in fact that I created a plan and a route that would take me there, it was 50km and 1000 metres climbing. So it would be much tougher than biking the seaside flat way.

I told my self I would decide based on how I felt after visiting the ruins.

I left the campite and went to the ruins.

The guys who I talked to yesterday at the parking booth were still their as they promised and I was able to lock my bike as I went to the ruins.

I entered the short line up to go into the ruins and I was standing beside two British people who looked about my age.

In the 10 minutes we were in line be became friends and decided to walk around the ruins together.

Seeing Pompei was really interesting. I have never seen such a large ruins. It was an entire city and you can kind of image how life was when you walk through the streets.

Many of the 2000 year old artwork was still with color on the walls of the ruins.

I will let the pictures I took tell you some stuff.

If you don’t know Pompei, you can google it for more information. But basically in 79 AD, a volcano erupted nearby ( the one I want to bike up) and the ash from the volcano killed everyone and buried the city of Pompei completely.

Many years later they discovered this buried city and the ash had well preserved everything.

Then I left the ruins, grabbed lunch with my friends, and they left back to Napoli.

I laid in a park for 4 hours napping. When I woke up I realized I would have no time to climb the volcano after all. I would have to rush the 40 Km flat route in order to make to the hostel in Napoli for checkin.

Oh yeah, I booked a hostel in Napoli. I felt like talking to people again and maybe going out for drinks. Taking it easy and rejoin the regular world loool.

So I biked and biked fast. I was feeling antsy. I biked as fast as I could just to test myself a little bit.

I arrived in Napoli and its a massive city yet again. It took me forever to find where I was going.

I happen to stop at once point since something fell out of my bag. And This lead to a conversation that almost ended up in me sailing a sailboat from Sicily to Napoli…

Now I am on standby as a crew for sailboat chartering.

I got to the hostel and it was soooo nice. I could shower, there was a bed, a kitchen. Luxuryyyyy. It’s funny since this is the nicest hostel I have ever been in. But many of the people staying in the hostel think that it is terrible, and the worst one they have stayed in. It’s all about perspective I guess. I have been sleeping in lemon trees…

I felt so out of place with the bike. Many people I met had just started traveling or only take trains. They all usually visit the same places as well. They all have the same shocked reaction most of the time when they ask me and I tell them how my trip has been so far lol.

Even on day one I met some amazing people in the hostel, and Argentinian friend, two girls, on from Latvia and one from France. We all had amazing conversations and probably will go explore Napoli together in the coming days.

I forgot to take a photo of the room of the hostel. Ill help you image it instead. It was a hostel bunk bed and I had the top bunk. There were about 4 other bunk beds in the room. Everyone was from Australia in my room.

That’s all for now,

Enjoy the photos
