#33 - Unforgettable day on Corfu

June 21st

A day trip to Corfu.

I am writing this on the ferry returning from Corfu to Igoumenitsa, where Adam and I have spent the day biking. I have a view of the sunset to my left, and Adam and I are sneakily drinking our beers on the front of the ship. We made a fantastic dinner with limited things, it cost us 7 euro, i’d say it’s pretty healthy. I’ll get to that at the end of the post.

View from writing seat

If I did not already mention it, Alex is currently studying for and finishing off his last exams. He is studying mathematics in Greece and has two exams left. One was today (at the time of writing this he has finished one of the two, so he has one only left).

Since we planned to return to Igoumentisa in the evening, we did not need to bring most of our gear, so we just ran with one light bag that had electronics and room for food and drinks when we biked around Corfu.

On the 8:30AM ferry

We enjoyed the ferry over on the sea I listened to music sung some songs and stared out at the close by Greek islands. It’s always amazing to be on the sea.

I took pictures of the Giant yachts I saw, thinking one day it would be nice to own one - really just cause. I know I dont need a big yacht but if I could have one…. Why not?

So many tourists on the ferry. And I dont know why exactly but I feel so far removed from the traditional tourist. I feel like I connect more with the Greeks here now than the passer-biers from other European countries.

Adam and I say hi to everyone here in Greek, we even talk in between ourselves in a mix of Spanish and Greek. But here in Corfu, none of the tourist reply to use when we say hi in Greek ONLY THE Greeks. (And one Chinese family we saw said that to us and we were so happy).

We got off the ferry and cycled aimlessly around Corfu. I asked Adam to tell me a random number between 1 and 10, he said 8.

Streets of Corfu

“Okay, so that means we stop for coffee at the 8th coffee shop we see.” I said.

We wanted coffee so thats how I decided where we would go.

We biked around the city counting coffee shops.

Corfu is beautiful, It looks way different that anything we have seen in Greece. In the past is was an island conquered by the Venetians (people from Venice Italy) so it has this Italian style too it.

This felt like the most touristy place we had visited. There were so many French people. Every second street I was hearing French.

One interesting thing is in the residential areas of Corfu we say that everyone hung their very colourful clothing in the street, above the small alleyways. This was crazy to me. Every Greek home I have been in has a washing machine, but NEVER a dryer. Very interesting…

When we finally arrived at a coffee shop we got doulble cappuccinos and sat in the shade in the afternoon heat. Once we finished we talked with the two students who were working at the coffee shop and asked them what we should see in Corfu while we are here for the day.

We left the conversation with a bunch of waypoints on our map for things to see.

One of which was the Old Fort. Ill throw a picture of the old fort that I took from the ferry below.

The fort from the sea

Normally tourist pay 6 euro to enter to see it.

“When you go to the old fortress. Go to the ticket booth and say this in Greek “Πάμε για μπάνιο (Pame ya banio)” and you might be able to just go in or free”

Turns out the fortress is right beside a beach where you can swim, which only locals do and know this.

I said in Greek, hello how are you. The ticket man said he was. good, Then I said “Pame ya banio”

I replied with the classic “ne” or yes in English and we walked by to the fortress without paying at all HAHA. We were shocked the trick from the coffee people worked.

We walked around the old Corfu fortress. It was cool, but we were not that impressed. At this point it’s pretty hard to impress us hahaha.

But we enjoyed the fortress and especially the fact that we did not need to pay, since we are on a tight budget here.

Yes I know that it’s dishonest - but we are students on a tight budget, we usually decided where to eat a restaurant depended on the priced of the food being too high by 1 euro. Each euro counts! Plus if they asked us to pay we would have. Or if the magic words didnt work we would have. I just wanted to try he local hack haha.

View from the top of fortress

Ethics aside let’s continue.

We left the fortress and biked to the next point, it was a cool part just south of the main city.

Swimming in Corfu town

We hit some amazing trails! With the new gearing and no gear on the bike, we climbed some really technical rocks and roots on our bikes. It was really fun!

At one points I biked up this gravel trail and two Greek men stopped me. One with a Camera.

He asked me some questions, he was impressed by the bike - I think he thought I was a lunatic.

But he and the other man started to interview me with the camera. I answered some questions and afterwards it was all over he said that it was for local television.

I guess I am on local Greek television. Yippyyyyy, I am famous now.

He took my email and said he would send the clip when it goes on air. Let’s see if that actually happens lol. It was probably the worst interview ever. He asked me how I got here. I said I biked here. He asked me what I thought of the garden. I said it was nice. That was pretty much the interview. I’ll be surprised if they actually airs it haha.

We left the trails, biked some more around Corfu, and we took some pictures when we found a good spot.

Next big goal of the day was to bike to the other side of the island to go to one of the best beaches (According to trip advisor) in Corfu. We just needed to bike 20 measly kilometers to arrrive there. (It was called Gyflada beach)

So we biked and biked up and up till we got the the beach.

Here are some pictures

My only comment about the ride to the beach were we took a route with a lot of up hill, but biking up hill without all the bags and gears was easy - I hardly broke a sweat with the easy gearing and no bags.

When we arrived at the beach we were surprised that the trip adviser “Nicest beach” was only okay….. if you compare it to the beaches we have been spoiled with each night in Greece lol. It was very crowded.

Anyhow, we sat in the sand and drank our 1.5L bottle of wine we bought for 2 euros in Corfu old town and relaxed in the sand.

I knew Adam pretty well before the trip, but spending this much time with someone you start to learn even more. I enjoyed chatting over 2 euro beach wine with Adam.

Then craziness ensued…..

Maybe 20 feet from where we were sitting two people pulled an unconscious old man out of the ocean.

I won’t go into too much detail, but it appeared as if an old man had had a heart attack while swimming, very close to where Adam and I were sitting. Someone pulled the man out of the water and he was laying motionless on the sand.

Quickly people swarmed, a lifeguard came, and CPR was performed.

I wont go through all the details, but the CPR did not help, and IED came about 10 minutes later and that did not work either.

At the very end an ambulance took away the body.

Adam and I watched the man die, assuming he was not dead before he left the water.

It was hard to watch but we were okay. I had never seen a man die before and I don’t want to again.

I was hoping he would make it but unfortunately he did not…..

It was a sobering experience, this old man didn’t have any family with him at the beach, no body know who who he was.

People crowded around, I even saw people taking photos…

So…. Now I have seen Corfu, and had an unforgettable experience on Corfu.

Soon we will arrive back in Igoumentisa, and sleep in Alex’s moms apartment another night.

7 euro dinner and I am very satisfied on ferry back

I am very grateful to have met Alex, and Adam is too. It feels like we have a Greek cousin and are his family visiting Greece for the first time. We are getting a true Greek experience. Both Adam and I think that it’s unique and beautiful. We never could have planned something like this…

I wish you all a great day, take care and enjoy the time we have.

 

As it turns out when we got back from Corfu, Alex mom had mis is traditional cheese pie.

It was delicious, and we were very happy :)

With love,

Erik
