#34 - Kai’s grand return.

June 22nd

I missed the post yesterday due to being a little busy with some stuff all day. Anyhow here is the 22nd. Ill keep posting things as fast as I can :)

Enjoy.

June 22nd was a day trip by car around 150 kilometres into the mountains to a larger city Ioannina in Greece (Around 100,000 people).

Alex had to write his last exam to finish off the year at the University in Ioannina.

The plan for the day was to see the city after he finished his exam. So early in the morning we drove through the mountains to Ioannina. I slept almost the whole way in the car.

Car ride in the mountains

When we got to the University, I continued sleeping while he took his exam.

Once his exam was over, we then went around the city.

The streets and part of the castle.

Ioannina is beautiful. The city centre is built around one large Ottoman castle from when the Turcs occupied Greece and at the side of this castle is a 30km wide lake. To top it all off the city is completely surrounded by large mountains.

The lake

I loved it.

First thing we see when got to the city was another bike tourists bike.

But wait a second. We knew this bike! It was another cycle tourist from Switzerland that we met in Methana almost exactly one month before!

We soon stopped the car and got out to chat with him. He was equally surprised to see us. Yet another Methana person in Ioannina.

This was the famous bike tourist that carried the giant stick for protecting himself from dogs and was the first person we met who told us to go to the south of the Peloponnese.

We exchanged stories for a while over Giros (Greek pita and meat). We all thought it was amazing that we ended up at the same city with two extremely different paths to get there. When we met him in Methana he was catching a ferry to Athens.

After that we went back to the car and Alex took us to see a missive cave. We thought it was a god idea to dodge the heat and explore it.

We payed 3 euros and got a 45 minutes walking tour of a 2 km network of caves.

Here are some pictures. This cave was a walking cave compared to the last one on Mani, when they took us through a cave by boat.

One cave picture

This cave felt much much larger and to me, more impressive.

It was a nice 18 C in the cave which was great since outside it was 35 C.

We then went to the castle.

Outside of the castle spaced about its perimeter, were 35 art exhibitions. I took some photos of my favourite which I will share.

Not an exhibit but I loved the photo

One was a digital rhino on a big that moved and interacted with its environment. It was life size on the screen which loud speakers playing its rhino noises which made it feel like it was alive.

Rhino on the screen.

Another I liked was a digital screen of a flag on fire. I thought it contrasted well with the lake water behind it in the background.

Fire flag and lake.

After this we saw the castle and some cool view points of the city and lake from the top. Then we later in the shade and napped nearby. One of my favourite activities.

Eventually yet another thunderstorm came in and we fled the rest spot and went to the car.

We drove to the bus station and waited for a while.

Kai spent the last day on a bus from Patras to Ioaninna to come meet us all again.

Kai comes to meet us

We waited there until his bus arrived and had another epic reunion.

We somehow fit Kai’s bike in Alex’s tiny car. We had to keep taking apart his bike till it finally fit inside.

Somehow we fit it all in the car.

After dinner, we went to a good viewpoint of the city - there happened to be a rap concert going on. So we watched Greek community bands and wrappers play music with lots of other people around.

Local Greek rapper.

It was fun.

Eventually we went back to Igoumenitsa late at night and we all slept in the next day.

Now we are all back together.

Until tomorrow,

-Erik
