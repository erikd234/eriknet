#36 - Ciao Bello - The Worst and Best Goodbye

June 24th

This morning I woke up in the comfort of Alex’s childhood bed in his mothers apartment.

And now, It’s 1:30AM and I am writing this post on the outside top deck of a night Ferry heading to Brindisi, Italy from Igoumenitisa, Greece.

It’s nice. I smell and feel the sea breeze of the Aegean Sea. And soon I will take a long sleep on the deckchair of the top floor.

A big pro of having my camping gear is I have my sleeping mats and sleeping bag with me for a cozy outdoor sleep on the top level of this boat.

Oh yeah… and I am all alone. From the next while I will be solo travelling through all of Italy.

I have left Alex, Adam, and Kai in Igoumenitsa, and they will probably all leave their own separate ways in the coming days.

Let the adventure begin… Again.

 

How did I get on this ferry?

The day started in slow motion. Yesterday nights trek through the canyon river was exhausting. So we all slept like logs.

Reminiscing in my ferry tickets from the islands.

We basically did nothing all day. We just talked and talked about everything as we usually do when we are together. We cooked more pasta, and relaxed in the apartment.

All four of us have this thing where we can talk and talk for hours about the stories of our trips, the little moments, and all the crazy coincidences that seem to keep happening to us. Lots of conversations about life too - you know how it goes.

Everything feels so special and perfect - even the worst moments seem to have this serendipity to them. Like they were always meant to be that way.

During these deep chats we had together I realized something…

I want to go to Italy now.

I have been learning Italian for 6 months and it feels like Italy is calling me. Every morning this week from the balcony of Alex’s apartment I see the ferries coming and going from the port to Italy. Each time I thought… what if I just went there now - saying goodbye to the Albania plans.

And I did it.

I bought the night ferry to Italy and told everyone I would be leaving to Italy now. They were excited for me of course. I don’t think they were surprised at all.

Adam and I had long ago talked about some point splitting up since we want to see slightly different things. I want a deep dive of Italy and Adam wants to hike up mountains for weeks in the Alps.

I spent the afternoon preparing myself for the journey, cleaning my bike up, packing my things, leaving behind some things I never used on my bike so far and no longer needed.

Once I was all packed up, Kai, Adam and I went for a short bike ride to get some beers and go to the beach. Alex went out to see a friend and we were going to meet him later.

Biking around Igoumenitsa with some beers

We just biked randomly in one direction until we found the first beach.

The first beach

Once we got on the beach we found two other bike tourists, a young German married couple!

When bike tourists see each other on the road its always so exciting. You can relate so easily. You know what they experience and they know what you experience. The stories are always great.

We had the best conversation with them. Our conversation lasted about 5 hours. There were many many things talking to them that I thought were funny coincidences.

  1. It was their first day in Greece, it was my last.

  2. They came from Brindisi Italy, I was going to Brinidisi Italy.

  3. I said earlier in the day that it would be nice to meet someone who had camped in Italy, they had camped all over Italy and told me so many great places to go.

  4. They alway love sitting in remote places were no one can find them, and people always seem to find them - this time it was us that found them.

  5. We told them everything we learned about biking in Greece, useful phrases and locations - everything we wish we knew when we started there.

Talking till the sun went down

After our conversation we all left together and I bought them beers. It was my name day today (A Greek Christian celebration for the name Erikos) - and tradition says I have to buy beers for everyone on these occasions.

beers

Then Alex arrived with his friend, they met and we all went together for a final pizza meal before I left.

pizza

The pizza was good and I ate until my stomach hurt. Now I have as baseline to compare with Italian pizza. Let’s see who wins haha.

At this point it was 11PM and my ferry to Italy left at 1 AM in the morning. I had to get there about 1 hour before since it’s such a big ship and there is security and a checkin. It’s much more serious than the ferries that stay within Greece.

So I left everyone at the pizza place to go back to the apartment alone to gather my things and shower. All with only 1 hour before I needed to be on the boat.

I said a goodbye, but only a quick and superficial one since we planned to meet at the ferry terminal before I left in an hour.

I rushed back and packed all my things, I said goodbye and thank you to Alex’s Mom who was at the apartment.

And I rushed full speed with 5 minutes left to the ferry check in.

But wait… my friends were no where to be seen to say goodbye at the terminal. I was being rushed by the staff and they kept telling me I need to get on the boat NOW and that it will leave soon.

So I had to get on the ferry without saying goodbye……

The distance between the entrance to the terminal and the actual ferry docking station was about 800 metres. As I biked, to my right, there was a big fence where you could see the road. I looked along the fence to see if I they would show up to say goodbye while I rushed to the ship.

I was hoping to see them again once more before boarding the ship.

I got to the boat without seeing any of them.

I was a little disappointed as they scanned my ticket and usher me to the boat….

Guess I don’t get to say goodbye.

In this moment I thought

“oh well, That’s too bad I didn’t get a proper goodbye.”

And

“Goodbyes are overrated anyway” (my inner self talk, i was actually disappointed, but quickly accepted it for what it was)

Then Kai called me.

He said they were all there and trying to find me. I said I was already on the boat and it was too late…Giant steel gates, far away from the boat, would prevent them from entering without a ticket. I did not think I would be able to get off the ship.

I walked to the back, where I entered the boat, and all the staff were very focussed and busy ushering the last trucks on the ferry.

I said to Kai on the phone I will try and get them to let me to run off the ship to the fence and back to say goodbye just before they leave.

Problem 1 - I cant explain that in Greek to the staff at all.

So I got Alex on the phone call and was about to give the phone to the guy who got me on the ferry.

I asked him in Greek if he speaks Greek and he looked very offended and said I only speak Italian, in Italian.

I was like in my head “shit okay, Italian time”

So i just explained that my friends, were over there on the distance behind the fence and if it was okay if I ran over to say goodbye. Somehow they understood my horrible italian haha.

But even still…

he said, “NO we are leaving now.”

I was persistent and he slightly gave in and said.

“Go talk to Maria”

then I ran to a girl name Maria and I did the whole thing over again in Italian

SHE also said,

“no you cannot go there now we are leaving.”

I said just 5 minutes and I run over really fast to say goodbye.

Another miracle happened - she understood my Italian again.

And she let me run over to everyone at the fence to say goodbye.

I ran with my sleeping pad and bag in my hands the 400 metres to the fence with my shoes half on and my shorts falling down (For some reason my shorts were on loosely… My hands were full of things I was carrying so I could not pull them up as I embarrassingly ran across the port).

Then I made it to the fence out of breath and we got to all say goodbye to everyone through the steel fence.

I said goodbye to Adam, Alex, Kai, Isabel, and Isabel, and Daniel (Alex’s friend and the two Germans we have the 6 hours of conversations with).

Kai gave me my underwear that I had forgotten on his bike and Alex gave me an Ice tea to drink.

What a way to say goodbye. I wouldn’t have expected a goodbye to be any different for us. It was completely in character for us and our trip - every day feels like we are in some kind of movie LOL.

I went back to the boat with the biggest smile on my face.

Now everyone on this boat seems to think I am Italian, and when I am walking around the boat or asking for information from people I do not know when to speak to people on the ship in English, Greek, or Italian.

The fence and port from the top of the ferry.

So here I am, back on the ship in route to Italia - all alone :)

Molto molto bene.

So, today I decided on a whim to go to Italy all alone. My time in Greece has come to an end.

I think I need to sit and process it for a while. So much has happened.

Two questions you might have that I am asking myself right now.

  1. So 1 month in Greece, what are the takeaways?

  2. Why have you left Adam and decided to travel solo?

As I mentioned before I am too lazy to keep both a hand written diary AND write these long blog posts about the days. So this is a two in one. I like to type with a keyboard rather than hand write. It’s just easier.

You get to see everything and see what I am thinking, and I get to look back at this in 10 years and have a good laugh.

To answer the first question.

Greece is amazing. It was an unknown country to me completely, and I now I feel like I understand what it means (at least a little bit) to live in Greece. I have seen the way of life of many people here and have a deeper appreciation of the culture, people, and the country as a whole.

I have written many things in this blog about things I noticed about the Greeks crazy parking habits, the pride they have for their national hard liquor drinks Ouzo and Tsipuro (Both spelt wrong for sure), the strong interest in politics held my many students, or the many other things I have noticed about Greek tendencies that are not coming to mind right now.

Most of all, I have met amazing people in Greece. Alex felt like a main Character in the biking across Greece adventure. We kept meeting him at checkpoints like an adventure video game. If we did not meet Alex I think we would have been in Greece for only 5 days, but I was in Greece for 40 days! Absolutely crazy. Meeting him was a complete fluke. Destino.

Lastly, I have learned that people are capable of incredible kindness to people they don’t know at all. I never held any pessimistic views about people, but this trip has even further given me hope for humanity.

Question 2 I will answer another day. It’s time for me to sleep as it’s getting cold on the ferry. I will go into my sleeping bag now and go to bed. I pray it doesn’t rain on me.

Stay tuned for next time where I answer why I am now travelling alone.
