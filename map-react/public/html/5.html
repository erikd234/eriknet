#5 - Greek life; it is hard to leave Methena

Wednesday 24th

Hey,

We woke up on the island and we decided that we do not want to leave Methena just yet. We are still staying with Alex in the villa.

Going to the store and beach chilling.

It’s really nice here and there is so much to see on the island.

We just spent most of the day relaxing. We biked to lo stores on the island and bought food.

We made some pizza from scratch, making the dough from flour and yeast. We bought cheese to make our own Saganaki, which is Greek Cheese.

It’s really hard to leave here. There is so much to see on the island. The road biking here is amazing. There is no traffic and all the hills coastal roads have fasts descents, and great views. Im seriously contemplating biking laps around the island.

Greek coffee with a view

I have been spending some time learning the Greek alphabet and saying some basic phrases in Greek, the language is actually very similar to Spanish and Italiano in some ways. The language sounds very beautiful.

Enjoying the islands beaches

The bakery we needed to buy the special ingredients for the pizza was closed so we biked to the beach to kill time. Basically just laying on the sand for an hour and taking it easy.

can not beat the biking in Methena

The hot spring man

On the ride back from the Groceries Alex yeld something in Greek to a passing truck driver. The truck driver got out of the car and came to talk to us. Turns it out it was the maker of the hot springs we stayed at. He had moved to the island after falling in love it on a bike trip 15 years earlier and now lives on the island. He built the tub we sat in, and he also built the pump that pumps the heated water to the tub.

His name was Elias. It was great chatting to him about some of the history of the island. He told us about 2000 year old churches or massive oak tries hidden in the volcano hills.

We left it there with Elias and continues back to the house.

watched the sunset on the windmill

Nearby the villa there is an run down mill, we climbed up on the ruins to watch the sunset. Very nice.

Our pet cat saganaki.

When we got back from the store we made a pizza and went to sleep early.

Probably going to stay in Methena for a bit longer.

-Erik
