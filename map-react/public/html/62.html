If you already forgot, we slept here last night at the top of some mountain in the Italian Alps ….

just wow.

I woke up several times in the night, we slept with the door half open for the view. Each time I woke up, I took a glance through the half open door at the stars. All the coulds had decided to sleep for the night aswell and were nestled beside all the peaks and ridges. It felt like we slept above a planet, I say “a planet” since it felt like we had left earth at this point since the terrain looked so different.

The first thing we did in the morning was do the hike down the ridge. Going down was much more scary than going up. The ridge as you may have seen in the photos is not very wide and has cliffs on both sides. I was doing my best to follow the rule “don’t look down”.

The nice part was all the ground was frozen. So all the loose rocks on yersterdays climb were frozen together and we could walk down sections that were impossible the day before in the afternoon sun.

Below is a picture of the glacier that was to our left all night. I really liked this picture I took on the way down so I wanted to share it.

The glacier

We stopped for lunch and made coffee with water from the glacier

When we woke in the morning we had drank all the water we brought. As a matter of fact, we didnt bring much water initially. In total for the 5 km (each way), 1000 metres elevation hour hike we brought 2 litres of water. This included for cooking and drinking on the hike. Kai only hiked with an empty coffee mug!

The reason for not bringing much water was because we suspected that we could drink the run off water from the glaciers we would be hiking beside.

So in the morning we made coffee and breakfast from the water flowing off the glacier :). All the water we drank tasted much better and more fresh than any tap water I have ever had.

Eventually we got down the trail and made it back to the bikes.

This is where we left our bikes overnight. It was hidden from sight. I dont think people could steal the bikes if they tried. I took alot of effort and pushing to get them there…

We then started on the trails.

The trails were insane…. Many parts were impossible to ride on the bike. Some parts were even difficult to WALK with my bike. I filmed this on the Gopro and I started the process of uploaded the vidoes.

About 30 minutes into riding the trails I was going over a difficult section and I was foced to put a lot of force onto the pedals to stay on the trail. I ran over this rock on the trail and suddly it became impossible to pedal my bike.

I had bent my brand new small the chainring on the bike. The chainring is the front gear that connects to the pedals.

I was no longer able to pedal. We managed to bend it enough to where I could pedal in the big gear only. This made all the hills I had to climb really difficult. I would have to get it fixed in the next town or I would probably end up being stuck in the Alps. It would be impossible to climb over mountain passes without the small front gear.

The the trails leaving proved to be challengeing… rocks, narrow trail, and my broken bike.

And then we biked down a red mountain bike trail. I say we biked. but only Kai could ride it as it was too difficult for my bike. It was extremely steep and rocky. I gave Kai the go pro camera and he filmed it. This will be on the youtube channel soon.

Part 1 - Passo Di Gavia - Kai on a red mountain bike trail in the Italian Alps

The way down was brutal. It took Adam and I few hours to go just that kilometre. We had to hike down with the bike for most of it and we were left very sore afterwards. There were small parts we could ride…

Eventually we got to the bottom and biked down the rest of the decent on the main road.

When we arrived in Bormio we went for icecream and beer. We used the 20 dollars the Romanian guy gave us the day before and send him a picture saying thanks.

icecream after surviving the trails

After icecream Adam and I said our final goodbye to Kai. He had to go visit some friends in the evening and he left us in Bormio. Adam and I needed some rest.

I then started on my quest to fix my bike. I went around to all 5 bike stores in Bormio. With each bike store I visited my hope for fixing the part diminished. They all took one look at the bike and told me they would not have this part.

Finally I went to the last bike store in the town. The bike mechanic did not have the part but he did not give up on my like others.

We went together put it on the bike stand and took off the broken part. The mechanic took a hammer and a clamp and bent the chainring back straight. Its aluminim and not ment to be bent back (this metal gets much weaker after a first initial failure), and it was not perfectly straight… But I went outside the bike shop to test it and it seemed to work… We will see if it holds.

I then met up with Adam for pizza. Bike seemed to be doing fine with the fix, thought I had lost confidence in my gears holding and was being very gentil with my bike.

The day ended with us eating in a pizza restaurant. The waiter gave us alot of sass. He got mad at us for drinking tap water. They said if we wanted water we had to BUY it.

We then went to find a place to sleep in the forest and found a lovely park off a trail a bit out from the city.

Bormio is beautiful and tomorrow we will rest here. Adam’s knee is really hurting him.
