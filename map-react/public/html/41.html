#41 - King of the hill

June 29th

June 29

I woke up early, made a Greek salad. For the FIRST time in Italy i was able to find cucumbers. I really miss Greek salad. I think that the food in Italy is amazing. BUT the food in Greece is much more healthy.

In Greece i was eating so healthy. Italy is good but pizza and pasta only is not the healthiest. Im gonna start searching some variety.

Continuing on.

Early morning was tough. I was super super tired. I thought I might fall asleep riding. But i wanted to keep going since I had a big day ahead and I did not want to bike in the heat midday. Every morning kilometre is much easier than a midday kilometres.

I then stopped at a bar and the espresso revived me. I felt great.

I was still feeling a bit of a low from the previous high of Matera.

When I was biking along a bridge, as two baby kittens came out onto the road in front of me while I was climbing a hill.

I stopped and gave them food and attention.

After this they climbed all over my bike. So adorable.

I continued on and they wouldn’t let me. The kittens were running behind my bike whining. My heart melted as I left the kittens and moved on. I was so close to emptying one of my pannier bags to take them with me. I thought really hard about it and almost convinced myself I could do the bike tour with the two kittens.

Views views views.

Then I took a lunch break with another incredible view.

While I was eating I noticed a beautiful beautiful mountain village on the top of the hill.

Lunch break spot

I sat there eating Nutella and break for a long time contemplating if I should go and visit.

The only problem… it was way out of the way. This route was twice as long and double the positive elevation.

I said screw it I’ll do it since there is a lake on the other side that looked like it might be good to camp on that night.

WOW biking down the hill into the valley than back up was incredible.

If someone sponsored me for a GoPro I will take videos and share them. It would amazing to have for the Alps. Unless you have biked down a mountain you wouldn’t believe how crazy it is.

How can I describe it other than sheer adrenaline, when you look your sides you feel like you are top of the world. I am so excited for the Alps and or Dolomites. I might take a train from some point in Italy to get to the mountains faster.

At home I bike down local mountains, Seymour, cypress, grouse. I have biked to whistler as-well.

This riding here, in these mountains in the central south of Italy completely blow all of these local rides out of the water.

From the village in the evening

So i arrived in the village and everything was closed of course.

Just one bar was open….

Remembers bars in Italy are coffee shops. So I sat in there for hours, just chatting and relaxing waiting for a restaurant to open at 7 pm.

Then others came into the bar. Two guys around my age speaking Italian. One was Italian and one was Argentinian. We spoke in Spanish. Then another man came in and ask for something. He was a backpacker with a big backpack. At some point he relieved that he was Mexican! Another Spanish speaker. So we talked in a strange mix of Italian and Spanish.

Many times I found myself talking in Spanish and then switching to Italian. I was having a lot of trouble keeping to speaking only Spanish.

We all went to dinner together afterwards. It was great fun chatting about our travels together.

Finally I left down the mountain village just before dark. Another wicked wicked downhill ride.

I arrived at the lake to find it was not even a lake. But a man made water reservoir. I thought it would be easy to get to but all the trails down were really steep and all locked up with fences and chains.

So I just walked in the forest a little till I found a nice spot and set up my tent.

As I slept around my tent were dozens of fireflies. I had never seen them before but there are so many here in central Italy. They flew all around my tent and littered the forest with little lights.

Beautiful.

Ciao,

Erik Dahl
