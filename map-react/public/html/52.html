July 10th

July 10th

Spark notes version

1. Biked all morning trying to get to Rome

2. It’s hot hot now. 38C in Rome.

3. I took a nice long shower with a garden hose facet for about 3 hours

4. I went really fast on the bike, I was pushing hard

5. I met my friend Egita 🇱🇻. And I got my towel back. She was surprised that I made it to Rome so fast.

6. Egita and I walked around Rome. Enjoyed walking by foot better than biking around. You see things better.

7. Enjoyed good conversation and the many beautiful things to look at in Rome.

Longer version.

I biked all morning. All roads leading to Rome (finally i can say this and it’s factually true)

Took all morning but after another 100km i arrived outside of Rome.

I was feeling gooood but once I got to Rome it got hot hot - 40C.

I sat under a cold water fountain for a few hours and washed up. Rome has many water fountains. What was interesting to me is that they are always on. They just constantly drip fresh cold water without any ability to turn off. Seems like a waste of water to me.

I then went to Rome and explored around saw the colosseum from the outside and everything from the outside.

It’s an impressive city.

Rome is different than I imagined. I only got to see a small sections of the city, but it was more spacious than I thought.

I loved the large plazas and historical areas.

It’s annoying how many people there are. I disliked the chaos of big cities. And when there are too many tourists like in Rome I think locals become less friendly.

Italian food is good but I came to the conclusion that Greek food is way better. More variety, more flavour, and less expensive.

I met up with my friend Egita for dinner and I got my towel back!

I left my bike at her hostel, they didn’t seem to mind it.

We had a great dinner and walked around the city by foot until late in the night.

It’s way way better walking around while it’s cool at night time.

Eventually we got back to the hostel I was gonna drop her off then go find a place to sleep. But when I got to the hostel I just passed out right away in a chair in the common area, at once point I moved to the floor with my sleeping pad, and finally I took a couch. Free night in a hostel.

No one said anything to me about sleeping there.

It was satisfying going from Napoli to Rome in two days. Good to know I can cover lots of ground when the road is flat.

Bye for now,
