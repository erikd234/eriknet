#70 Rumbo for Paris

August 5th

Welcome back,

I graduated. I am finished university! It’s a crazy feeling. What the hell’s next? who knows…

Rumbo for Paris.

Rumbo, if you dont know, I’m pretty sure means somthing in Spanish towards going towards somewhere. The part I love about it tho is when you said Rumbo you really get to roll that rrrrrrrrr. It sounds lovely.

So where did we leave off?…

Last time I posted I had was sleeping in Julia’s apparment, and played house husband for a day with Adam.

Julia had work early in the morning so when I woke up she was already gone. It was just me and Adam.

I took my time getting up and said my final goodbye to Adam. my FINAL GOODBYE (it really was this time… no more accidental bumping into each other like before). We had such a crazy adventure together in europe and now it was my time to say goodbye for the rest of the trip. Adam was off to Spain, and I was off to Paris to go fix issues with my flight home. On previous evenings I had spent HOUR and HOURS on hold with the Air France phone line, in the very end I found out the only way I could resolve my flight troubles was to go to Charles De Gaulle Airport in Paris and talk to Air France myself in person, mano a mano (more spanish, but this one is actualy an ENGLISH saying funny enough).

I left Julias appartment. Below is a picture of Julias shoe as I left the appartment. I wont explain why I took a picture of the shoe. Only both Julia and I know. Thanks again Julia for being such an amazing host. You made switzerland awesome :)

Julia’s Shoe.

I then left for the train station in BASEL. YES a train. I was done biking at this point. I did not have enough time or energy to bike to Paris if I wanted to do all the important things I had planned in other countries (forshadowing epic adventures in Amsterdam).

When I arrived at the train station. I asked the people who work there to help me get to Paris. They told me that it was IMPOSSIBLE to get to Paris that day…. Whoopies, I didnt realize I had to actually plan my transportation. I was so use to going with the flow, independly with my bike (No one tells me what to do when I am using my bike to get around).

So… shit. It was impossible to get to Paris, which was what I wanted to do… And I already said goodbye to Julia so I couldnt GO BACK to her appartment (that would be akward). So I improvised. I hopped on a train to Strasbourg instead. A year prior when I was learning French. I did a language exchange with a guy who lives there (he taught me french in exchange for english). So I sent him a messages and we planned to meet up and he was gonna give me a tour or Strasbourg that very same day!

So I bought a train ticket to Strasbourg. On the boarder of Germany and France.

Here are some observations I made about the difference between trains I took in Italy and trains I took in Switzerland…

First of all, taking the train in Switzerland was 100 times easier than Italy… They were SO HELPFUL in the station and it was way less chaotic. I also had lots of time to board the train with my bike and didnt need to pay extra to bring my bike on the train. It was a CHILL expereince, much different than italy where I had to SPRINT to the correct car in order to make it on the train and i had to pay extra for a bike ticket.

When I arrived to Strasbourg my friend met me at the station and gave me a tour of the entire city in about 4 hours!

The strangest thing I saw was that french people here called burritos Tacos.

Strasbourg is a beautiful city with a rich history. One hidden tourist gem was a bomb shell that landed in a building from World War 2 that didnt explode. Its still just sitting their in the building for anyone to look at.

A really cool church

Above and below are two of my favourite things I saw. One an amazing and beautiful church. and below, a view form an old military barricade that french soldiers would look out from to protect the city from germans, or something like that.

A beautiful view point

In the evening my friend and I parted ways. After spending the day meandering around Strasbourg, I was feeling tired and found a nice quiet spot in the forest outside the city. It was a rainly night but I found a nice sheltered spot in deep bush with trees to protect me from the rain.

I remember falling asleep enjoying the rain drops falling on the forest floor and the roof of my tent.

A picture of the campsite in the morning.

Thats all for now folks.
