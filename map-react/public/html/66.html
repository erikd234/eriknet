#66 - When everything goes wrong, what do you do? Life ain't real.

August 1st

Hey people,

August 1st was the hardest day of my whole trip.

It pushed me to the edge in both mind and body…

The funny part was it was pretty much self inflicted and I chose to feel this pain today.

I mean I didnt have to do it, at any point I could have stopped and camped earlier.

I was ON A MISSION.

well lets get into it.

167km ride to Zurich

I woke up on top of the mountain pass.

I woke up on top of the mountain pass.

It wasn’t as cold as I thought sleeping at the top of the mountain. Even without cuddle buddies I was warm all night. I think it might have gotten down to 4 or 3 degrees at night. I was sleeping beside this rock that sheltered me from the wind.

I woke up early and saw there was some light rain, I waited and waited. When it stopped, I quickly packed up and I went down the mountain descent.

CRAZY decent. Here is the video, I think I finally figured out the descent filming angle.

Decending 7000 feet on a bike.

I biked and biked all day. It poured rain on me for most of the day.

Once I was in Switzerland, I noticed some things:

  1. So many bike tourists! I went from never seeing them to spotting about 30 in one day. I talked to many of them, and they are all from Switzerland and all do mini bike tours around the country.

  2. Most of the gas stations don’t sell food like in Canada. They sell cars! The gas stations are also car dealerships.

  3. Switzerland is incredibly expensive. Prices of food items doubled compared to Italy. At restaurants, you pay at least 20 Swiss francs for cheap food. (1 franc is more a bit more than 1 euro)

  4. Switzerland has amazing intercity bike routes. You can get around by bike so easily. I was biking on protected bike paths 80% of the time.

Hey remember my good old friend from Greece? Not Alex the chess master, not the Patras friends.

The bike tourist I met on the start of the trip! The guy with the dog stick who I met who I thought was Kai. The paper map guy…

The SAME guy that we saw 1 month into the trip AGAIN in Ioannina, Greece on the border of Albania.

WELL his name was Lukas and I messaged him on Whatsapp. He was from Zurich.

Around 30 km into the ride, messaged him to see if he was around.

Turns out he was! He invited me to stay with him and his girlfriend's apartment for the night. The only problem was I needed to get to Zurich for that, which was another 137 km away.

I decided to go for it. I MADE it a mission to get to Zurich. I would ride ALL day.

PHOTO INTERMISSION (Before hell breaks loose):

gravel roads for kilometres and kilometers

Little does he know how terrible it will become.

You know I was screaming happily in this tunnel. Amazing echos.

It was a mentally challenging ride since it rained the whole time.

At around the 130km mark out of 170 km, something tragic happened.

My cellphone fell out of my pocket, and I ran over it with my bike. I heard a loud crunch. yup….

I stopped, in the rain, picked up my phone to check the damage, and it was entirely broken. I couldn't use the screen at all. Thankfully, I could use Siri and called my Swiss friend, Lukas, and told him. He gave me a spot to meet him in Zurich.

So now I had about 50 km left to bike without using GPS. It wasn't that bad, actually. I just followed the massive lake beside Zurich the entire way and didn't need maps at all. One time I remember to stop to talk to three swiss germans. I asked if which way was Zurich and they looked at me with the bike and thought I was crazy…

I stopped to ask for directions a few more times.

Every kilomiter hurt, I had given into the pain. I stopped to go to the washroom once and lost my warm blue snowboarder hat I had bought in the alps. That stung…

I got to Zurich.

Eventually, I arrived at the meeting spot.

It was a festival to celebrate Switzerland Day on August 1st!

At the start of the trip in Greece the swiss people I met TOLD me that it was a good day to be in Switzerland since it was their national holiday, I never believed I would make it but HERE I WAS. Damn.

I met up with Lukas, and we got some food at the festival, and I met his friends.

Then, THE UNBELIEVABLE happened....

Guess what….

I saw Adam's bike there at the festival!

It turns out that completely by accident, Adam and I had found each other in Zurich. We SOMEHOW got to the same TINY (this was a small small festival in Zurich that only really locals knew about) and somehow we both met up there AGAIN.

We met up at the festival and were both shocked that after saying goodbye a day earlier, I had found him there in Zurich. He had taken the train to Zurich through the alps when I had biked over the two mountain passes.

Adam had found his own little magic in the city and didnt need a place to sleep…

After the festival, I went with Lukas to his apartment and met his girlfriend. They let me use the guest room. Both Lukas and his girlfriend were doctors, power couple.

With my phone completely broken, I was still able to take a couple of pictures using Siri.
