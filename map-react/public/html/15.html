#15 - The last supper

June 3rd

Today was a do nothing day. I sat on the beach in the sun from morning till the afternoon looking at this as a view, switching between reading, swimming, studying Italian, and writing these posts

Life is hard here - hard to get out of this beach chair

We found out climbing gear rental was 50 euros per day per person so Adam and I said no thank you. I was also tired and thoroughly enjoyed doing absolutely nothing on the beach.

We decided we would leave Leonidio the next day - and at long last - leave kai behind. Kai is meeting a friend in the mountain ranges and will bike with them soon. Eventually, he will venture off on his own probably high in the mountains in Greece.

Adam and I really enjoyed biking with Kai. I think we will be meeting again on this bike trip. There’s talk of a reunion in the Alps or Dolomites for my birthday in July- we will see what happens.

When we first met Kai I had no idea I would be biking with him. We have spent over two weeks all together and it’s flown by.

Anyway, we made a big feast together to celebrate the two weeks we travelled together.

Yummy

We went out for drinks after to celebrate - reminisce in the memories, the good old days in Methana, the skulls in the little cave, or just biking up mountains with ease while I’m dying from my horrible gear ratio. (try your best to hold back your tears, it’s okay.)

After drinking in the little habour we moved over to the little beach side club and danced with some Dutch people who had sailed there. The club was empty until 2 am - that’s when all the Greek people started to show up. Greek clubing usually doesn’t start till 2-3 am 😅 - in Vancouver that’s when I like to be in bed by.

The club stays open to 11 am, we didn’t last that long.

Worlds best dancers

When I finally reached the tent at 5 am I could still hear the clubs music 600 metres away.

We have a big biking day up a mountain

until tomorrow,

-Erik
