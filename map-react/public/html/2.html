#2 - Leaving Athens, Introducing Kai.

Things just keep becoming more unpredictable.

Luck brining us to the most beautiful place I have ever been.

Hey all,

I am still catching up on these posts. I want to have something written for each day. I am currently writing this post listening to Suzanne Leonard Cohen, sitting on the balcony of a Greek Villa in a tiny mountain village. I'm still currently on Methana.

Hold on, I'm getting ahead of myself here...

Let’s get back to the hostel in Athens.

Sunday the 21st was the day after my “Night out” in Athens. I went to bed at 6 am and woke up at 10am. It was the checkout day from the hostel. Yes, I was tired.

Exactly on the theme of the trip. We had no plans where we were going to go. An hour before checkout, we looked at our map and found a campsite 85 kilometres outside of Athens in some mountain ranges to the left.

We started to pack up our bikes and then Adam and I bumped into Kai.

Kai was a fellow bike touring cyclist we met the day before at the hostel when we were drinking beers in the backyard of the hostel.

He had been travelling around Greece for three weeks. First impression of Kai was this man has been places and seen things. Getting to know him after the past few days has been eye-opening. He spent over a year and a half biking in South America, Columbia, Peru, Chile, Argentina. He also let me use his amazing folding keyboard to type this post. :) I think that I could write an entire book on Kai and still have a lot more to say.

Here are some interesting things about Kai.

  • He can whistle two different tones at once

  • Literally nothing bothers him. Most easy going person I have ever met.

  • He has a surprising story about just about everything.

Anyway, Adam and I standing there in the backyard of the hostel invited Kai to join us in bike touring since he seemed super interesting, and had some amazing ideas of where to bike in Greece.

Kai is from Minnesota, USA.

Kai joined Adam and I, and we quickly abandoned our previous campsite plan. Kai ended up leading us out of the city.

Adam and I biking through Athens' maze of streets two days before took two hours to go from the coast to the hostel. Following Kai, we got out of Athens in 20 mins to the port where the dozens of ferries take people from Athens to the various islands.

Biking out of the city with Kai and Adam was SOOO much easier. And also beautiful. We biked through completely different neighborhoods in Greece. All on a protected bike path. Which is much, much nicer than busy roads without bike lanes.

following Kai onto the ferry.

We got to the port side to see massive yachts and beautiful buildings. We headed along the portside roads all the way to the ferry terminal. Stopping for some Souvlaki (Greek pita style food) in transit.

We arrived at the ferry terminal without knowing where we were going and just got on the next ferry that came into port.

The ferry ride leaving Athens was amazing. I spent the whole time on the upper deck enjoying the sea breeze and working on my sunburn. The Athens harbor was filled with giant ships, and yachts. Various Greek islands in the backdrop. Packs of Seagels would fly with the ship which looked amazing.

Adam writing in is journal

After about 30 minutes on the Ferry, we arrived at the island of Aigina. Kai, Adam and I were lining up to get off the ferry when we decided to stay on the ferry to try and travel with these two Lesbian Spanish Flamenco Musicians who were going to the next stop on the ferry. The next stop was the much smaller, much quieter island of Agistri. Kai, Adam, and I are all three gringos that speak Spanish so we were very happy to chat with two Spanish musicians. We wanted to hear them play the guitar and practice Spanish. They also seemed like cool people.

We had tickets for the first island but no one really cared that we stayed on the ferry to the next stop, this small island. 95% of the people on the ferry got off at the first stop.

The next stop, Agistri, is mainly beach restaurants, a few small hotels, forests, and little villages.

The island was STUNNING, and the air was fresher outside of the big city.

Asking an Irish e-biker to take our photo.

We biked along the seashore, every few minutes I would say “This is the most beautiful place I have ever been”. Endless sea roads, with empty beaches and clear blue water contrasting with white Greek island houses.

The Spanish Musicians, ended up doing something different, they had a hotel and we were camping so we never actually got to hear them play music. It was a shame but none of cared in the slightest because it lead us to this mini paradise.

Sea roads on Agistri

Exploring the island roads, we ended up at an unbelievable campsite just a few kilometres away from the ferry terminal on the island. Even though it was only 8 kilometres, this completely removed the campsite from the rest of the island. And we had a quiet and peaceful camping spot on the beach.

Dirt road down to the beach

This was my second time camping completely on my own and cooking my own camping food. I feel as though I lucked out big time with the beautiful spot we had.

Here were the night's accommodations.

After setting up shop, we just started to camp out. And enjoyed a peaceful night on a Greek Island. The first night of many to come.

Talk to you tomorrow.
