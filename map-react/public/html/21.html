#21 - the day where everything goes wrong

June 9th

I’m writing this at the temple of Zeus in Ancient Olympia. Currently there is a thunderstorm going and 20 people are all huddled under the only shelter from rain in the archway of the entrance to the archeological site. I guess Zeus is angry.

Back to writing about the day where everything went really good - then really bad.

We woke up early so that we would to be first one at the cave, mainly to bike before it gets hot. When we arrived at the ticket booth at 8, there were already Germans waiting there with theirs RVs (this did not surprise me at all). We had to wait 2 hours before the caves would actually open up to let people in.

Entering the cave

We entered the caves and it was absolutely incredible. It was a 2km cave network and the take you on a small (and very tippy) boat through it. The driver has a stick that he uses to push the boat inside the rocks. The cave at times is only wide enough for the boat to just fit - and I many times had to duck to dodge rocks .

Somewhere in the cave

Inside the cave was interesting. There were 1 million year old limestone rocks that shimmered with the lights of the cave and rocks that looked like icicles.

They also had names for all the cave rooms such as red apartment, white apartment (which did not make any sense to me), Hercules’ rocks, and water 30 metres deep at some points.

I felt like a real tourist wearing the silly life jackets.

Tourist acceptance

When we finished in the cave, it was an hour later,l than when we planned to leave. we had to start biking in the middle of the day. We biking in the heat until aerepoli where we went to a taverna that was recommended to use by the chef of the cafe we visited on the other side of Mani.

When we arrived, it was closed, we were told to come back in 3 hours. I explained that we had received a recommendation for her restaurant and we bike there to eat specifically there. We showed her the name in Greek for what food we were told to try there. After that she changed her mind and opened the kitchen early just for us. It was sooo delicious.

Adam after sitting down

Pasta and lamb

At the end she brought us a free dessert too 😋

In the afternoon, after getting started again leaving Areopoli. Things started to go wrong.

We were biking down the first hill out of the city, and on a sharp turn while leaning my bike, my tires slipped out and I slid a few feet with my bike on the concrete.

I was okay. I got up and only had scrapped elbow and a bruised leg. We chilled for a while on the side of the road and cleaned up my scrapes and continued on.

My eggs I was carrying didn’t crack! And only one of my two tomatoes was smushed.

Reflecting on the fall, I leaned my bike too much for slow speed I was going at - and combined that with a lack of focus caused me to slip out. I got lucky that some scrapes is all I had and I’m going to be much more careful (more on this later)

Next thing that went wrong was while we were going downhill on another street climb, Adam olive oil he was carrying on the front rack of his bike exploded all over him and his breaks! He suddenly found it way harder to break. We were fortunately able to stop but we then spent an hour cleaning olive oil off the rims of his bike and his break pads - that stuff is difficult to get off when it gets everywhere 😂.

Here was the view while we did that.

olive oil view

The rest of the day was very pleasant- minus the scrapes, bruises and olive oil.

Following British guy

We got to a small port side town and followed a British cyclist through a bike path through one city then following the road right at the base of a big climb (saving it for the next morning).

Sea side road view

Some trip observations:

  1. It’s way way to hot during the day to climb mountains

  2. Out average day here is insanely hard - like biking up seymour, grouse and cypress (Local mountains in Vancouver, Canada) every day. It’s really really hilly. Up and down.

  3. We get baffled looks biking through the tourist areas 😂- and in tourist areas we always get smiles, waves, and “yaaaa” (which means hello)

Things go wrong in bike tours - and more things have gone wrong since this day im writing about haha. you will have to wait and see what. The only spoiler I’ll give is that we always find solutions.
