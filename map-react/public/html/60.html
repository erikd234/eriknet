#60 - Passo di Gavia (Gavia Pass)

July 26th

Hey everyone,

I am finally coming back to write about the rest of the trip. I took a long break from posting. Currently I am back at home, on a family vacation now just outside of Gambier Island just outside of Vancouver.

I have a computer now and and I have no excuse for not posting the rest of these. I have the pictures, the GPS ride data, and my quickly journal entries from the trip.

So lets get into it. Last time if you dont remember I had met up with Kai and Adam in the Alps. Ponte di Legno… I had survived sleeping in a house filled with unexploded bombs and reuinited with friends I was ready to explore some mountains.

Climbing the pass.

We woke up in the park refreshed and ready to go. Today was going to be a big day. We planned on going to the top of the mountain pass and sleeping somehwere at the top. We saw some trails on the map. Of course, Adam and I’s bikes dont have much trail traversing capability - so we had no idea if we could do the trails on our bikes. We were going to try anyway…

First things first, it was going to be cold up there. The weather forcased said it would be 4 degrees in Ponto Di Legno. The top of the pass was another 1200 metres higher so we were expecting sub zero temperatures.

Fortunatly there was a market in town open and I was able to buy a nice puffy jacket to keep my warm if it got colder at night.

The market

Next thing we did was go back to the bar for some coffee. I got the largest cappuchino of my life… I asked for a double and they brought me it in a giant bowl

Look at that large cappuchino!

After coffee and some partries we made breakfast number two, egg sandwiches.

the town of Ponto Di Legno…

We started our way up the climb and it was incredible. It was very difficult and steep, but I was well rested and felt fresh…

The views were amazing all the way up. This was the most spectacular riding in the Alps that I had done.

just woooow

Half way up the ride we stopped for lunch with an incredible mountain view. Across the road there were some construction workers.

We heard them yell something at us in italian.

“Vino! Vino!“

wine?

Drinking wine in our cups with our lunch view.

They were shouting wine at us and offereing us wine to drink. So we invited them over and they gave us wine as we ate lunch in the middle of our mountain climb.

Sitting there and eating lunch, we all felt like we were in heaven. We made some coffee and ate delicious bread, cheese, greek salad. The view, the food, and the wine was all so wonderful.

the lunch spot with coffee

We continued on the ride with more unreal views…

from the road.

finding some gravel to bypass a tunnel

Eventually we got to the top and there were building refuges for travellers at the top of the pass. You could rent a room or buy a snack. We had no need for either since we had food and a tent so we didnt stay around.

However even at this point of the day it was very cold at the top. About 3 or 4 degrees. I wanted to buy some gloves from the people in the refuge.

I tried to negotiate the price of the gloves. 20 euroes! for some crappy gloves. I only wanted them to move down to $15 and they would not budge on their prices.

I left the refugee with cold hands…

We continued on, we had a couple more metres to ride before we got to the trail we were going to take to find a place to sleep.

Then I see another refuge house. I went in and asked if they had gloves…

They didnt think so but they went to check. Moments later they came back out saying they dont have it.

Then I told them why I wanted gloves - we were going to sleep up there.

They looked at me like I was crazy and warned us that it would be especially cold tonight. It was frozen solid up there the last few nights.

I think they felt bad and looked again for gloves for me. They did not want me to freeze.

The came back with a snowboarding toque and gloves.

I said, “how much? $15 for the gloves?”

They responded with. NO no no… 5 for the gloves and 5 for the hat….

So by going just 100 metres down the road to the other refuge, I got a hat and the exact same gloves for half the price!

I was very happy and warm when we started riding down the trails in the valley.

The valley trails

Riding in the trails was intenseeee. There were times where I COULD NOT FALL or I would probably die. Since to one side was a cliff. The trails were rough but possible for Adam and I. More mental focus is required. I always love getting off the roads with cars…

I have this all on go-pro footage and one day will figure out how to post it. Should I edit the footage or just post all the raw footage? What do you think?

After expereincing pure joy and happiness in incredible trails with amazing views in the valley we got to a place we decided to camp.

wowow…

We cooked some food, pesto pasta and after tomato pasta. We then set up one tent. We were going to sleep all in Kai’s tent. We thought it would be cold and three people in one tent would help us all stay warmer. Again my sleeping bag is only rated for +7 degrees and it would reach below 0 degrees…

I put on all the clothes I brought with me. I had 3 shirts on, my sweaters and jackets, and all my pants and shorts.

We slept the night and it was nice and warm. In the midde of the night I looked out the tent to see everything was frozen and white. It got below zero for sure at some point. I still don’t know how cold it got.

This was a fantastic day and once again I said out loud. “This is the best day yet, I never want to leave the Alps, Okay I will spend the rest of my trip here”

As always stay tuned to see if I ever leave the Alps.

This time I wont make you wait a month for the next post.
