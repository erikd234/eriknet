#12 - Epidaurus Theater

May 31st

Hope all is well.

I’m writing about today, tonight. Looks at me on top of things a little bit. Turns out you get a lot of free time on a bike tour.

Today I was super tired and sore from the giant day in the mountains. So I wanted to take it easy.

We had lots of riding to do I just went slow and took our time the whole way.

I told myself in the morning.

“No gravel, just easy roads”

And of course the first thing I hear from Kai is.

“We should take this gravel shortcut”

Shit.

I just said yes let’s do it since it turns out it really was a shortcut to get to this Ancient Greek theatre. Usually Kai’s shortcuts are twice as long and twice as hard through trails.

So we went on the gravel road. It wasn’t nearly as hard as the day before, and I was happy to dodge the mountain highways a little.

The gravel road was taking us to the ancient theatre. But on the way, as usual, we stumbled upon another archeological site, completely empty of people. We climbed a small fence and walked around this ancient sanctuary that was supposed a place of healing for thousands of people long ago. Lots of cool ruins here. I always wonder when I see the ruins… what did the think look like before? It would be amazing to see.

Part of the ruins we found

Ancient sleeping hall

We left this one and continued to the main event.

The super special Greek theatre - (Epidaurus Ancient Theatre if you want to google it)

From the top of the theatre

The acoustics were amazing.

We continued on, eventually the roads just became downhill FOREVER. We had maybe 20Km of nice and easy downhill, and some fast roads too. We finally got to enjoy all the fruits of our labour from the previous days climbing.

Long slow downhill

We ended our ride some kilometres later at a campsite in Mykines. We are going to look at another famous ruin temple thing tomorrow.

Here were tonight’s accommodations - a campsite this time.
