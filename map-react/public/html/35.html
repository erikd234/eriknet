#35 - The river that takes souls to the underworld.

June 23rd

Im loosing track of how many days I have been in Igoumenitisa. Yet again, keep reading to see if I every leave, or go back for another lap of Greece.

We relaxed and did nothing all morning.

With Kai, Alex, Adam and I all sitting on the porch chatting and looking at the ocean I cant help but feel like I am back on Methana. So much has happened since then. I feel like Adam and I have changed and learned a lot of things already - especially from Kai.

Alex mom made us a delicious spinach + feta pie.

Nina cooking the yummy pie

In the evening we decide to do a hike.

It was not an ordinary hike.

It was walking down a river nestled in a mountain valley for 5km to a small village.

It was a special river in Greek mythology. This is the river that the dead travelled down to go to Hates, the underworld. A boat man would carry the souls of the dead down the river.

We drove 45 minutes to the hike. Itâ€™s hard to get there. The start of the hike is over a narrow mountain road with winds and twists, and the end of the hike is in the village. Since we had only one car, and the start and end of the hike are at different places, it maked things complicated.

Mountain roads to the river head

Our idea was this, Alex drops us at the start of the hike, in the mountains in the middle of nowhere, he drives back to the village (very far away) to park. And Alex then finds some way to get to us by hitch hiking to us. Once we would arrive we would start the hike.

Here were the problems with the idea.

  1. It was 5:30PM and the sun sets at 9PM, and the sun is lost even earlier in the giant valley we were in. It was at least a 3 hour hike.

  2. It might take him potentially hours to get someone to drive him to the middle of nowhere, back to us.

  3. We had no cell service at the start of the trail so we just had to trust Alex would find his way. (The saving grace was that if he never came there were a few people camping in the valley with cars we could rely on in an emergency)

We tried it anyway.

Alex dropped us off and we started to wait. I relaxed in the afternoon sun by the river.

River left. And the old house.

There was an abandoned house across the river.

I climbed up and took a nap on the roof in the sun for a while.

Nice nap spot

We waited an hour and a half and eventually Alex arrived back to us.

He told us the story for how we got to us.

After about 10 minutes waiting he found a truck driver who would ride the route for him. This guys name was George Winter (epic name) and drove really slow through the winding mountain roads in his large truck, then dropped off Alex close enough to walk down into the valley where we were waiting.

George Winter had epic stories for Alex about his bike touring days (I think Alex mentioned he was with bike touring people).

We started the hike and it was great. We just walked and walked through the river.

We all had sandals on which was perfect for this hike since since we had to do a lot of walking in the river, it would have been terrible in shoes.

Walking down the river

Another side point about shoes. Is I have brought shoes on my bike tour but I have almost never worn them, one 2 times at the beginning of the tour, but since then, never. I think I have only worn sandals for the past 26 days or something. A part of me wants to send them home. Iâ€™ll keep them just in case, but it is an interesting tangent. Sandals are just the perfect shoe for everything the biggest advantage is no smelly feet from socks all day and your feet keep really cool temperature wise.

Okay back to the river hike.

The river got harder and harder to walk down. We had to do some easy rock climbing and step down small water falls.

river got slightly more techn

Eventually we came to a part that was completely impassible. It was a 3 meter waterfall and it looked very dangerous to jump in.

Before the hike we heard stories that you at some point need to jump off a waterfall to make it further down the river, but this looked way way too sketchy to be the spot. So we were left very confused about how to continue.

Then we started to follow what looked like a trail. But it quickly turned into us getting lost in the bushes heâ€™s high above the river canyon. We climbed and climbed very steep tracks. It appeared as if we were following a trail made by wild boars.

It starts to get dark and we maybe had 20 minutes left. We used offline maps to see where we were in the bush and made our way down to a trail through thick thick bushes and very steep upward and downward hiking.

We had to climb down a tree for one section.

Somehow right before dark we found the trail again right beside the river. That felt good since we all had accepted the fact that we might be bush whacking for a while in the dark.

Once we got to the path we back tracked to the the thing we most wanted to see. The intersection of the hot and cold rivers together, this was the famous river that takes souls to the underworld.

Now it was our turnâ€¦

The best way was just to float down the river, so we all did. For the next two hours we floated down the river in the dark. Through a canon and small waterfalls. If it got too fast in the water we stood up and walked around, if it got too shallow we did the same.

One point we stopped and took photos. I love these photos.

Souls in the river

Floating down the river in the pitch black, with nothing but the moonlight was amazing, I felt at peace (except when we had to stand up for the rapid sections and waterfalls that looked to sketchy). These cannons and rivers remind me of the ones near my house, the only difference was the water here was not ICE cold, so we did not die of hypothermia like we would probably have done if we did the same thing at home.

We finally got to the end of the river and made it back to the car. That was the craziest hike I had ever done - probably. Mainly since we floated down a river in the dark and got lost in the bushes.

It felt amazing to get back to the apartment. I fell asleep right as i hit the bed.
