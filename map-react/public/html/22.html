#22 - Missing the bus.

June 10th

Adam and I are really enjoying camping beach to beach. It’s completely free and we have the ocean to do dishes and go to the washroom 😂 (don’t worry we do both of those at different times).

It’s also great to have a shower in the ocean as well - it really sucks going to bed covered in sweat.

Now on to the longest day so far with the tour.

we really wanted to be on a beach again.

Anyway, we woke up at 6 AM, to get a Headstart on the day and to skip biking in the crazy heat. And our plan was to get to the big city, Kalamata well before 4 o’clock, when they would be a bus that takes us from Kalamata to Patras. it was supposed to be a short and easy day, just 30 km, but with only one big morning climb, 400m elevation, over 14 kilometres.

midway up the climb

We made our way up to climb slowly, it was a great idea to start earlier made the climb way easier.

Kalamata

We got to Kalamata around 11:30, and while we were eating Souvlaki for lunch, we decided to not take the bus but instead biked Patras - our map said it was only about 250 kilometres.

So we kept on biking - we planned to do the 250 km over the next couple days. The only challenge was that to get to a good spot on the beach, we need to bike another 70 km, with another 1000 metres of climbing.

The ride was fantastic,

We biked through Kalamata, it felt a lot like biking through Athens, busy and crazy everywhere (this time we found a bike path for most of the city).

Once we left the city, we had lots of flat gravel farm roads, completely empty of cars.

At one point a lady yelled at us to stop from her house. She told us that the road ahead was dangerous and that we should bike on the busy highway instead.

We asked her why

“Gypsys”, she said.

As several normal looking people came out from the road we wanted to go on. We asked that makes them dangerous out of curiosity - and she couldn’t tell me what made them dangourous.

Anyway, we wanted to be respectful so we listened to her. But when she couldn’t see us anymore, we took the next turn off and continued on the “dangourous road”.

The road was actually amazing. It was one of the best gravel roads I have ever biked on. The “Gypsies” were about the equivalent of the homeless people in Vancouver, but they were all smiles and waving as we biked by.

The highway we would have to take was really busy and full of cars and trucks - so we took the risk and went with the Gypsies.

One time during the ride we found a “short cut” that left the road and cut off a few kilometres.

We had some serious trails haha

Turns out it was a super steep and difficult rocky trail / and road and it took us an extra two hours 😂 - we even got lost temporarily and accidentally biked through someone’s olive tree farm.

The trail biking was amazing. When you bike trails you are so focused on weaving through rocks that you forget the pain from the climbing. On the road all you feel during the climbs is the pain 😂. Also my bike is not made for trails at all - it’s really uncomfortable haha. I wish I had bigger tires.

Epic roads

100 kilometres later and we arrived to the sea with a beautiful sunset. We were properly tired - the stopped at the first beach we found - and the first thing I did was get off my bike and jump in the ocean.

We cooked a big dinner and set up tents and went to sleep. Over the next two days we will bike about 160 kilometres and arrive in Patras🥳.

camp spot

Until tomorrow.
