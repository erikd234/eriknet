#65 - 2 ups and 2 downs - finally I have brakes that work.

July 31st

Hey yall,

Its been a while since I wrote, I find it hard to sit down and write these now that I am back into the swing of things here in Vancouver (Yes I made it home)

I am going to keep posting tho. little by little. (Plug for my other newsletter here).

Lets get started.

Twist and turn: Passio di Stelvio (P1)

I woke up in the morning, at the bottom of the beautiful green valley beneath the passo di stelvio. You can see how we got there in the video above. I didnt edit it its just the raw footage. Email me back to tell me how far you get in the recording. Im curious haha.


Adam and Erik part ways once again…

We woke up nearby the lake in the park. This was the day that Adam and I would be saying goodbye to each other for good. His knee was hurting and he decided he must stop biking. He would continue to Spain in the next few days by train.

We said our goodbyes and continued on. He left the lake side lawn we were relaxing on before and I was left there… All alone. I felt weird in this moment again. I was back to making all the decision.

I stuck around for a while doing home housekeeping. Reorginizing the gear on my bike, throwing out garbage, I watched a youtube video and learnt for the first time how to change the breakpads on my bike. This took my hours and hours as I countly figure out how to tighten a key component. Eventually I figured it out and my brakes became SO MUCH BETTER. I had been strugging with break problems for the entire trip and having new pads made an incredible difference in my ability to stop my bike. I felt more confident again going downhill.

Another house keeping item I took care of was my inflatable sleeping pad. I had a few nights in a row that I would wake up and all the air would be gone. I suspected that there was a leak. Again I was watching youtube videos on how to find a leak. I tried spraying it with soapy water… no luck. I then reluctantly submerged the entire inflated pad in the dirty duck pond water.

I know, I know. I wasnt happy about that either…

Guess what. I was wrong there was no leak at all. I just didnt have the cap securely sealed for the last few nights of sleep. whoops. I washed off the dirty duck pond water the best I could, but I dont think my bed ever smelt the same…

The Lake where Adam and I seperated.

I biked up a mountain pass in the morning

Avoiding the highway by taking gravel trails

Incredible views of course. I’m loving the alps.

I entered Switzerland and it felt like the world all the sudden changed. First of all now everyone speaks german to me, I guess I look swiss or german. The other biggest change was the price of everything. I was out of camping fuel so I tried finding some. The only place I could find it was in a camping store.

In greece a can of gas was 4 euro. Here it was 20 euro! And it wasnt even the correct gas type for my stove. Its all I could find so I took it. I need to be able to cook somehow. I could cook with it so I was grateful for that.

Sitting and waitin for the camping store to open

Now we got into the hard part. Today I had one goal. Go 90 KM. If I made it to 90KM then I would have gotten over two mountain passes each of which reaching over 2200m of elevation

I biked up the first pass. It was hard lol. I can’t even remember what I was thinking this day. I just biked in silence for hours, I remember it being particularly brutal, and feeling slow on the bike.

When I reached the top of the first pass of the day I met a swiss history teacher who was bike backing in the alps for two weeks.

We biked down the first mountain pass together and had some good conversations. Lots of people in switzerland lovve bike touring and bike backing.

These downhills were incredible. The roads were wide and the descents were not too steep so I could go FAST. I loved every second of the downhill.

Its a thing with biking, on the way up you question why the hell you ever decided in the first place to start biking, then on the way down you remember just how awesome it is.

The mountain roads

I ended my day on the top of the second pass. Sleeping at 2300 m of elevation. Tomorrow I will enjoy the decent

On the way up one of the climbs

I convinced myself I need to get to Paris and hurry up my trip. I only have about two weeks left. I am on a mission to bike to Paris in the next 4 days. For me it’s go go go mode. (its 800km to paris…)

More of the roads.

I went down and said goodbye to my new swiss biker friend. It was nice riding with him for a little bit. I got to the base of the second mountain pass at 6pm. It would probably take me 4 hours to get to the top of this climb. AKA I would have to somehow sleep up on the top of this mountain, or go down a bit to find a place.

I just decided to go for it. I started climbing and climbing the mountain. This was a steep climb and long. About 16km and 1100 metres of climbing.

I kept climbing and sun kept going down and lower.

The valley below the pass.

It was beautiful.

The sun kept going down

Eventually I got the the point where it was cold and dark, and I still seemly had a ways to go till the top.

I made the choice to stop and cook on the side of the road in the dark. I made… what did I make? I think some quinia and some zuchinni. I was so hungry, cold, and tired. During the day I was fueled on bread and cheese, and I rad run out.

I finished and continued biking in the dark to the top of the mountain.

eventually I determined I had arrived at the top, and I was best off sleeping there and leaving early in the morning. Going down the mountain in the dark probably was not the best idea.

I set up my tent at the top of the pass and had one of my favourite bootleg camping spots, maybe 10m away from the road over a little ridge that protected me from lign of the site of the road.

Iphone’s night camera (pitch black at night sleeping at the top of the pass)

I set up my camp in the dark with my new headlamp (I forgot to mention I bought one in the camping store with the fuel).

That was awesome having a headlamp.

Where I put my tent during the night. The rock protected me from the wind.

Sleeping up here felt wild and beautiful. Afterwards I felt like I could do anything i set my mind to.

Thats all for now.
