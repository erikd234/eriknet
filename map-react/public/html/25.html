#25 - The reunion

June 13th

We woke up on the floor of the restaurant at 6am. The floor was very comfortable and warm all night.

Yesterday I left my bike helment in Kostsos bike shop in Pyrgos. So we need to bike back to his shop then go up to Patras. We used a routing app to find the best route, and we found one we thought we could do from Ancient Olympia to Patras through Pyrgos.

Big day to Patra from Olympia

We needed to stop in Patras for multiple reasons.

  1. In Methana about one month ago we ordered electronic keyboard that we could use with our phones to make writing these blog posts easier

  2. In Patras there are many bike shops and there was a possibility I can get better gearing to make biking up the hills easier. I have a 30 teeth on my smallest front gear and 36 teeth on by biggest back gear. This gives me a ratio of 36:30 of about 1.2. The higher this number for gear the easier climbing hills is. I get more help from the gears. 1.2 is fine if I have no heavy bags, but my bike weighs about 50-60kg with all the bags on it. For reference, Adam had a 1.8 gear ratio and Kai has a 2.2 gear ratio! They can climb up walls with their bikes. A 1.2 ratio makes it way way more taxing on my knees. My goal in Patras was to get a smaller front gear to make the ratio higher. The parts I needed are very hard to find for my bike. I have a front gear system that they do not make anymore - and also three front gears which is less common. We have many more mountains to climb.

  3. We found out that Alex is in Patras staying with friends! He would help us find a place to stay in city somewhere. We wanted to visit Alex again and catch up. It had been a few weeks since we left him in Methana - too bad Kai did not make it Patras as well.

We left the restaurant in perfect order. Making sure not to leave any mess for George. We wrote a small thank you note and headed out.

We had very quiet roads all the way to Pyrgos. We biked the same way we did the day before. Going to Olympia just turned out to be a 40km excursion. It was out of the way but well worth the visit.

We got to Pyrgos again and I went to Kostos bike shop to get my helmet. Of course I told him about how awesome the tires were working and I thanked him.

We left Pyrgos following a small road, on the map it looked like it would take us right to Patras (It was not so easy as it looked). It was not that busy with cars, small amounts of traffic most of the way. We made sure not to take the big national highway.

Starting on the road to Patra

The road was beautiful once we got in the middle of the Peloponnese. We started to climb slowly and we had great view of lush valleys. This side of the Peloponnese looks so very different from the other side. The best part is its is much more flat. The hills and mountains are much smaller.
