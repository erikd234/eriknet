#64 - The highest mountain pass in Europe

July 30th

Morning wake up sleeping on the park bench.

Waking up at the park bench

It protected us from rain all night. I remember that Adam was complaining since the only hole in the roof happened to be right above where he was sleeping. He was slightly dripped on by the water all night long.

The beautiful think about camping in the alps is that there are no mosquitoes! Atleast here when it was raining. I got zero bug bites. I think I only would find them near the thick thick forests but this park and most places didnt have them.

When we woke up in the morning and were cleaning up our camp and an italian man appeared (as they usually did) and came to us and asked us if we slept there.

At first I wasnt sure if he would be angry or was just curious but before I could think what to say Adam blurted out a “yes we slept there” last night.

Turns out he was not angry at all. He was kind and curoius about our adventure. After a short conversation he invited us for coffee in his nearby horse stable… It turns out the man owned a stable right beside the public park.

Then we met his horse.

His horse

We enjoyed a nice conversation. He only spoke Italian so I did most of the talking, but Adam’s spanished seemed to help him undertsand atleast 70% of what we were saying. I would transalte when he looked really confused.

After a pleasent coffee conversation talking about the history of the town we were in and the park we stayed in (that he had seen being built since his family owned that land for multiple generations), he ended up giving us wine at the end and sparking water after the coffee.

The wine in the hut (we took it with us)

We have a picture of the hut below. He also offered for us to stay in the hut if we needed a place to stay that night. We politely declined since we were ready to climb the next mountain later this day.

his little hut beside the stable.

his horse roaming the park

After this we set off to the hotspring we were told about the other day. It was a decisous for us - rest another day in bormio and sit in a hotsprint all day, or just leave over the stelvio pass.

We did the third and worst option… Dehydrate ourselves for a few hours in the hotspring THEN go up the longest and highest road climb in europe (30km climb 1400m up the entire time).

We showed up at the hotspring and took the advice we got to bike the opposite side of the river and walk across. It turned out to be a great tip since the usual trail is not possible to ride your bike on. The water temperatue was about 40 degrees, powered by some thermal vents, and there was a nice cold river to the side of it so we cycled hot and cold submerssion for an hour and a bit.

Biking along the river to the hotspring( See next photo)

And of course we ate cake and the gifted sparking water in the hot springs, we did not open the sparkling wine we were given, we wanted to save that for when we got to the top of the mountain later in the day.

Eating cake in the hotspring

 

Cold plunge cycles

After this we started the biggest climb I have ever done. Stelvios pass. It’s the highest bike pass in Europe with an elevation of 2988 metres at the top of the pass.

It was a tough climb - especailly after hottubing. My legs were dead from the beginning hahaha. I was tired from the hiking two days before and we also had decided for some silly reason to go a pretty fast pace. Adam and I had a little competition for who can lead up the hill.

On the way up

We went up and up and it was absolutely stunning.

We didn’t take many breaks.

The million switchbacks we climbed

There were photogrophers who sell you the photo after. He didnt buy my photo of him.

When we got to the top we drank the wine and we were given and we cooked a nice meal. I took a nap in the sun in the peak. I was surprised how many stores they had at the top of the pass. I suppose since it is so famous for the Giro Di Italia and being so high up it attracts a lot of tourists and adventurers.

a cool building up there.

Us right before going down hill (about 3 or 4 degrees up there)

After that we descended the mountain. It was 45 minutes of riding downhill! Incredible. A windy decent for 35 kilomiters with an incredible view the entire time of the mountain valley below.

I filmed it all with the GoPro and it will one day be published. Its a long video I wont edit it since I dont have space on my computer to edit a video of such a size. I will just upload the raw footage to youtube soon :)

After the climb Adams knee started hurting really bad. He’s decided he needs a longer break from biking and will be taking the train from the nearest train station. We had 5 nights travelling together and this will probably be the last time I see him until I am back in Vancouver.

We then went to the train station to help Adam figure out where he needs to go.

After this we went for pizza :)

We looked in the map and found a park with a large forest area.

It was really easy to find a quiet camping spot.
