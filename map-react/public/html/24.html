#24 - When a meal turns into a place to sleep.

June 12th

Thanks for all the replies in the last post. Yes i get all the emails! It’s great to read what you think. Please reply as frequent as you like. Iif you read something and it gives you thoughts you feel like sharing - let me know.

We woke up on the incredible beach as usual. Beaches make the best campsites as I have mentioned. The night before it was another one of those mosquitoes nights. Each time I would kill three mosquitoes on my leg four more would instantly come to replace them. Thankfully in the morning all the mosquitoes were gone.

The beach

Here are some shower thoughts about Greece before recount the 12th

  • Greek men say “Of course” a lot. Anytime you ask them a question they say it. This sounds kinda funny like they expect you to already know it as it sooo obvious that they know how to spear fish. Of course!

  • Olives and beer go really good together. Eat an olive and take a sip. Repeat.

  • There are soooo many dogs. They pop out all the time at us while biking its like a video game.

  • Lots of people smoke in Greece and I found this contradiction below in a restaurant. A no smoke sign and an ash tray beside each other as everyone in the cafe was smoking except us

    No smoking but theres an ash tray

Okay on with the day.

Our only mission for the day was to get my bike tire fixed. We needed to bike about 35 km to do it. My bike tire and tube only needed to last that far - then I needed to find a bike shop in the city (Google helped with this) and find tires that would fit on my bike. (I would take what I could find).

The first part of the bike ride was a little annoying. We chose to take quiet roads instead of the highway but those quiet roads quickly turned into gravel roads. As I mentioned yesterday my rear tire had an air leak - and while it was slowly leaking, the gravel road’s impacts quickly pushed out more and more air.

Every 10 minutes on the way to the bike shop I had to stop and put air in my rear while with my hand pump. I think I burned more calories that morning with the hand pump than actually biking.

Basically all morning it was frustrating gravel roads with a flat tire.

Gravel sheets

We finally got to the city and found the bike shop. Back to biking in Greek cities its much harder. I think Adam and I are now comfortable in the hectic driving and I have made some strategies for not getting too lost in the maze- like streets.

My failed tire.

The bike shop was great. It was called Kostos bikes and it was a small shop. The owner of the shop only spoke Greek and he was the only staff there. When we entered the shops I showed him the tire he instantly got to work.

bike on the stand

In Vancouver, you have to wait forever for service and its expensive. In Greece it was instant. He did everything we could to help me.

He went to see what tires he said would work and I had two options - a smaller tire and a bigger tire for more trails (30mm was the small and 38mm was the big).

With the big tire we knew it would not fit with the fenders (the things that block dirt spray on the bike that go above the tire) and we also did not know if it would fit without the fenders on.

Even so I said lets go with the big ones since it would make the many gravel and dirt roads so much more enjoyable.

The tires fit just barely. They turned out to be the largest size that my bike could functionally support- wooo hoooo. I had to ditch my old fenders, but well worth it (I would just get more dirty in the rain and mud).

While Kosto was working on the bike we met his friend Emiliano, who spoke English very well who was also in the shop. He was a bike racer from Pyrgos (The city we were in) and shared with us all the bike routes he would do in the city. He also convinced us that we NEED to go see the nearby (20 km away) Ancient Olympia - where lies the ruins of the temple of Zeus and where they hosted all the Olympic Games in the name of Zeus.

Unfortunately it was in the opposite direction of where we wanted to go (it would be like going backwards) but we decided to take an extra day and see it - or at least go for a quick visit and bike back to Pyrgos afterwards.

After the bike was finished Emiliano showed us the best cheap Giros near by to eat for lunch. Giros are pita and the meet from the spinning meat cooker thing.

I left the bike shop very happy and I got the best and most helpful service from a bike shop ever. He cleaned my bike, lubed the chain and checked many little things on the bike.

new tires on bike (after riding trails later)

I bought some tools and bike lube that I was missing, got new tires and tubes, got a full service - all for 100 euros. This would be much more expensive in Canada.

We left Pyrgos and soon hit the trails and farm roads that would take us to Ancient Olympia

My life was changed with the new tires. It was the best feeling ever riding on the gravel roads. It was like playing the video game on hard and switching to easy mode. It was like treading water with weights and taking them off afterwards. I could not wipe the smile off my face and stop telling Adam how much better gravel riding felt.

I had grip on the road and on the trails, everything became easier (expect climbing on road hills due to more frictions, this became much much harder haha)

After some of the most peaceful and fun gravel roads we got to Ancient Olympia.

We went to all three of the archeological sites, the two museums and the ancient city where they had the games, the Tempe of Zeus, and more things. here are some pictures I took.

Column of the temple

Many Greek gods - told temple roof relief.

Famous statue

While at the Temple of Zeus, there was a flash thunderstorm and heavy rain. Fitting for the god with a lighting bolt. We all hid under the entrance of the Archaeological site packed like sardines with 30 other tourist dodging the rain. I wrote one of these blog posts while I was sitting in a puddle there. Free shower.

Once it started raining we decided we would not go bike back to the coast to camp but instead stay in Ancient Olympia - we just did not know

where. Of course, right as I get rid of my fenders it started to rain. Murphy’s law.

At around 8 pm we showed up at a nearby campsite. The rain had died down. When we talked to the owner he said all the campsites where flooded and no tents could be pitched there. So we negotiated a sleeping spot on the floor near the registration for a few less euros.

We left the campsite to go for diner, we just biked back to the village. Plan was drink some beers and eat some Musaka (Really yummy Greek food that is like Shepard’s pie.)

We went from restaurant to restaurant trying to find the best price. On one of the restaurants I looked at a menu and a Greek man came up to me and giving me his pitch for why his restaurant is the best.

I tried to negotiate the Musaka price down by one euro to match other restaurants - he came back for reasons why his restaurant was the best, free pita bread, desert, and salad.

He convinced me and Adam and we chose his restaurant.

We ordered the Musaka and had a delicious 3 course meal for 10 euros. He did as he promised and we got the delicious pita with hummus and feta, a sweet salad, the Musaka, and a brilliant dessert combination of Greek yogurt, honey, cinnamon, and apple. This is a recipient I will be recreating later on the bike tour.

couldnt wait to take the photo - we had to eat it first.

At the end of the meal the Greek man, George. Who say our bikes from the beginning asks us where we are from why we have bikes with so much stuff. We told him and chatted for a while.

Then he asked us where we were staying, we said a campsite, and he offered us a place to sleep in the restaurant for free. We would sleep on the floor of the restaurant with our sleeping bags and be protected from rain and we dont need to set up our tents.

So we said yes! And spent the evening in his restaurant, they gave us discounts on beer and wine while we took it easy until the restaurant closed for the night.

Chillin in the restaurant till closing

The rest of the night went like this.

  1. Adam and I left the restaurant to go to the washroom the the back alley.

  2. In route Adam and I met a class of students from Spain.

  3. We talked for a while and went for drinks in the little town. They were all so surprised to find two Canadians sleeping in a restaurant that both happen to speak Spanish

  4. Now we have friends in Northern Spain :). I hope we get to go there.

Yet again I was surprised by the kindness of people. It was amazing that George saw us and offered us a place to sleep for free. He said next time we come and we can stay in his tree house he is building. I guess we will have to come back to Ancient Olympia one day.
