#29 - the advantage of biking in a thunderstorm

June 17th

Wow today was an absolutely incredible day.

And guess what…. We left Patras :)

I’m picking up more and more Greek, I mean i talk like a 5 year old baby but I think I pronounce most of the sounds correctly I am able to read and write all the Greek letters. Add I have many basic frases down and I can make simple sentences to be understood.

Where is the … bathroom, store, super market,

Can I use the

I like this

Do you like this?

I can say “I speak a little Greek” or “say nice to meet you” and ask for names.

Im writing in my journal all the Greek words I learned and I have several pages of Greek words written down.

Greek is a beautiful language and it’s going to be hard for me to leave Greece and stop learning Greek. Who knows it might be the next language to learn

On to June 17th.

We left Patras. We felt eager to move again. Today was simple - just bike north. We didnt look at the map at all for the first 50 or so kilometers since we just knew we need to follow the coast and go north. The ride out of Patras was amazing an it felt wonderful looking out at the Peleponeeses, the massive mountains in the background and knowing that Adam and I had bilked over them.

The ride out of Patras had a spectacular view. It’s probably just better to show the pictures.

Peloponnese in the background

Coastal highway

We biked and biked, just the usual, I listened to podcasts, music, and thought about life. Eventually, we got hungry and stopped for lunch. I was tired and I took a short nap under an olive tree. Adam had more energy so he continued on and we split up.

We planned a little route together - somewhere we could maybe camp on a beach another 50 kilometres away.

We said we would meet there or I would bike faster and catch Adam along the way.

I napped for about 30-35 minutes and started biking with the afternoon sun alone.

Adam and I bike a lot together, but it’s also nice to sometimes bike alone. You get to go your own pace and choose your own routes. The are both nice from time to time.

Then lighting struck. Out of nowhere it was raining buckets and I was biking on a farm field in a lighting storm. I left the field I was in and bike towards taller trees. The disadvantage was I was biking in trails and getting covered in mud.

Thunder clouds rolling in

I think the closest the lightning got was 3 km away. Though I had to bike in the direction of it to make progress to the beach. I remember biking and a giant fork lighting bolt was write ahead of me (3km away from sound delay)

Eventually the storm went away and the rain stopped. I passed a small tavern and stopped and got some food and wrote yesterdays post and an amazing google review for the bike shop in Patras that helped me upgrade my bike.

Stopped at the taverna - bike looking coool

I was there maybe an hour, so I was about an hour and a half behind Adam. I figured I would see him at the beach which was about 30 km away.

I took a detour through some town and when I got to a gas station I was shocked it see Adam there. He told me he was filling up water and took a wrong turn at one point. It was just a complete fluke that we both had made mistakes on the route in a way that we came together again.

We biked together for maybe another 7 kilometers when another rain storm hit. This was was heavy heavy rain. We instantly looked for shelter where we could stop.

We saw an elevated concrete house with cover underneath it and a Greek teenager sitting underneath it. He whistled and waved at us and we decided to go closer.

i asked him in greek if it was okay if we come here to hide form the rain and he was very happy to have us there. He brought us chairs and offered us water.

He was 15 years old did not speak English, so we had a very basic conversation and then I would use google translate to ask him questions.

We sat and talked for while until eventually a truck pulled up to his house.

“Familia” he said.

His Mother, Father, and older brother got out of the truck and he introduced us.

After introductions, when they saw the bikes and we were from Canada, they offered us Greek coffee. We said thank you and yes.

Coffe and chocolate pasta

A few minutes later they set up tables and chairs for us and gave us coffee and this thing called Chocolate Pasta (that’s the Greek direct translation it was like ice cream)

We sat there over coffee and had a conversation in Basic Greek and English with the help of google translate.

“Φαγητό?” (fagito) they said

That’s one word i know in Greek. It means food!

They were offering us food. We were happy to accept and moments later they brought out Bread olive oils and risotto. We ate the food they brought us and continues our conversation. After we finished they even offered us more but we were so full we couldn’t eat any more.

They made us risotto!

They asked us where we were staying and we showed them the beach. They said it was too far and we can just sleep in the town center of the village. We could sleep under cover there from a church archway and they said everyone in the village would be okay with this.

We said goodbyes and took a photo.

Our Greek teenaged friend then lead us using his motorbike or the spot where we could camp. He was crazy with the motorbike doing wheelies and drifting. We were so shocked haha.

Following him on the motorbike

We got to the square and hung out with him and his Greek friends that appeared out of nowhere. It turns out there isn’t much to do for them in the little village so they enjoyed teaching us more Greek swear words. Malaka Malaka (the only Greek swear word you need)

Once they all theft Adam and I went across the street to buy a beer from a little taverna where old Greek men were sitting outside on the patio. (If I did not already mention thats the hallmark of a great coffee shop, old Greek men smoking outside it with beer and coffee)

We walked in, saying out Greek, Hello how are you, etc haha. They always look so surprised when you talk in Greek and are obviously not from Greece.

Right as we entered the taverna the one older gentlemen that could speak basic English was talking to us. He bought us both beers.

The all day coffee and beer shop across the street

We sat there taking with him on the patio, then we moved inside for the mosquitoes were attacking us, and we spent several hours in Greek conversations with the owners and workers in the taverna, exchanging English and Greek, and even learning some Albanian. Some of the people in the taverna were Albanian.

I learned a lot of Greek words and tomorrow morning Adam and I will go back to their shop since they offered us coffee in the morning before we start cycling.

We left the taverna saying goodnight, everyone parting ways with giant smiles. We then walked across the street to our fancy hotel room under the archway of the church.

Im gonna sound like a broken record here but people are increíble.

We got free dinner, dessert and beer. I only wish I could speak Greek better to learn more about all their lives. The waiter in the taverna has 300 goats and the family who made us dinner has about the same amount. Incredible.

How does this make me feel?

If I had 1$ for ever time someone told me Greece was dangerous to bike through (many locals say this lol) I’d probably be incredibly rich. Well actually I’d have like $30 or something.

All I see or receive is kindness from people. I’m still digesting this. But it makes me want to give more to others, pay this kindness forward.

Wow I wrote a lot today. I wrote this first thing upon entering my tent after leaving the taverna. If you made it this far. bravooooo. I appreciate you for caring enough to read it all. Let me know what you think what happened today. It felt incredibly special and I hope me writing this down helps me to never forget it.

Here were the night accommodations, in the middle of the village under the church archway.

Luxury hotel room

Until sooon.
