Rome in a day.

Both Egita and I wanted to keep travelling together for a bit.

She had reservations in other hostels in other sites. So together we had to see Rome in a day.

Which would be possible except for the extremely terrible and stupid awful lines outside of everything that usually is about 1-2 hours.

For the colosseum it was a 2 hour wait even thought we got there before it opened. And it was a wait in the blazing sun without shade. Pure torture. And the best part is you have guided tour salesmen offering line skips for a more expensive price. They target the most uncomfortable looking people at the back of the line

We held strong and did not pay.

We finally got in and the colosseum is very interesting. I liked it.

Then we went to the Vatican by bus.

We got a ticket from the Roman police for not paying the 1 euro bus ticket. You have to buy them in separate stores and we did not know this. We got a 54 Euro fine. He was nice and only gave us one ticket or pay instead of two.

We finally ended up ubering to the Vatican.

Waiting in another terrible line for the main church. 1.5 hours to get in. Most incredible church I have ever seen.

The Vatican puts every other church I have seen to shame. I couldnâ€™t believe how luxurious it was - lots and lots of money went into its construction I am sure.

The next stop was the Sistine Chapel. I really wanted to see the artwork on the roof.

Unfortunately we missed the last entrance time to the Sistine chapel by 5 minutes. I was disappointed but this gives me another excuse to come back to Rome. I will have to visit the Vatican museum another time.

Finally we enjoyed the walk back to the hostel, we grabbed our things and took the train together out of Rome to Florence.

It was my first time taking a train in Europe and it felt chaotic. We had 5 mines to get the train and I had no idea how to do it with the bike. Then we got lucky and the train was delayed so we had an extra 20 mins to figure it out.

Finally when the train arrived I had only 3 minutes to get on. And find my train car all the way down at the end of the train. The furthest away. Then of course as I put my bike on the train the door closed and smashed on my with the bike. I had to battle the door to make sure me, the bike, and Egita made it on.

But once in the train it was relaxingggggg wow. So much easier than biking ðŸ˜‚. Dangourously easy, and inexpensive.

We went to Florence, arrived and it was way nicer than Rome. Just because it was so much less busy. It felt calmer.

We went to her hostel. And I once again went in. They had an awesome room balcony on the building. This time I asked the receptionist if I could sleep on the balcony.

He was from Argentina, so we connected over Spanish and talking about Argentina. I have never been to Argentina it love the people I meet from their and I want to go.

We talked for a while about various things, until he just offered me to stay in the hostel for free since the bed was not being used anyway.

So I stayed in the hostel in a bed again without needing to pay :) I could use a shower and everything. Very very nice.

I slept like a babyyyy in the hostel bed that night.

I went out that night before sleeping to walk around Florence with Egita. Again much cooler at night.
