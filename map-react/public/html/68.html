#68 - Swiss watches marking time, As we're floating down the Rhine

August 3rd

Hey, welcome back.

I woke up on the forest floor surprised to see about 50 slugs all over my tent. Even some had made it inside of the tent. It seemed like I slept in a nest of slugs if there exists such a thing.

The slug habitat

I went over to my bike which was outside the tent, and it as well was covered in slugs. Probably the worst part is my brand new running shoe I bought for the trip was completly COVERED in slime.

The day before, I had lost this shoe’s brother in Zurich. I was holding on to this one incase a miracle happened and I found the lost shoe. Well, after seeing this shoe covered in slug slime I decided no miracle would happen any longer. I threw the shoe out as soon as I could. Bye bye brand new runners

One again I was biking on bike paths all day. Biking between towns in Switzerland is so dang easy. I followed a sign that said “bike to Basel” for around 80 kilometres to Basel.

Adam slept somewhere in Zurich the night before on the riverbank of the Rhine. He was aready on route to Basel when I woke up that morning. My plan was to see him there at the end of the day.

We went to Basel to visit our friend Julia we met in Greece months earlier.

Julia, our swiss friend we met in south greece.

If you don’t remember her, we met each other in Greece about a month ago in Mani, Peloponnese. (See the blog post named Coincidence Cafe (https://erikdahl.beehiiv.com/p/19-coincidence-caf)).

to be honest, I didnt think I would ever make it to Basel, but it worked out not being difficult to get there and directly on the path I wanted to take.

Okay back to Switzerland…

On the road to Basel

I arrived in Basel and met up with Adam by the Rhine river (which runs through Basel too). We started to eat some food we bad bought from the grovery store.

We sat there and ate some food. Julia was busy so we were killing some time till the evening when she would be free and we could meet up.

Sitting by the river in basel

Then….

A friendly swiss girl around our age walked by with her bike and started talking to us.

She was around our age, from Basel, and was after a short conversation we became friends and decided we would all float down the Rhine together.

Here in Basel the Rhine is a long river that flows through the entire city. Many people start some point up the river and float down for a kilometre or so before getting out. We wanted to try it.

In order to do this we had to get a floaty sack to put our valuables. In so we didn’t leave them up the river unattended.

To solve this problems, Batina (our new friend) said we could go to her apartment close by to store ours stuff and grab the float sack for valuables and clothes.

We then biked with her through Basel to her apartment.

An appertment in Basel

After this we went back to the Rhine and floated down the river.

I was in shock that after 15 minutes in Basel, Adam and I had once again met a stranger who took us on a little advetnure in their city. After 25 minutes, we were in somones appartment getting the river floaties. After 45 minutes, we were all fully submerged in the river floating down with the current through Basel.

They water was 20 degrees according to the sign so it was not that cold, but not as warm as the beaches I had last swam in in Italy.

We got a nice tour of Basel. Floating down the river you see it a little differently…

Maybe after 10 minutes floating we left the river and walked back to our bikes upstream… Then we grabbed our things from the apartment and invited Betina to dinner with us and Julia (our friend who lived in Basel that we met in Greece).

We all met up and spent an awesome evening together.

Basel Gang

I said this yesterday but Switzerland is so expensive! I paid 30 Franks for a small burrito and they gave me a 7 frank bottle of water without asking me…. It was tasty but that was the most expensive burrito I have ever had.

Adam and I stayed in Julias appartment that night. She had a couch and blow up mattresses for Adam and I :). Had an early night since Julia is a nurse and worked the next morning.

First day in Basel was lovely :)
