Listening to Elias speak to us about his home felt sort of like watching a show the the History TV channel. As it turns out Elias and Methena starred in a famous Greek Documentry. There is a lot of forgotten history here.

Elias was excited to share everything with us. There was a lot to see and explain. His home was filled with unique history that unlike anything I have seen before.

Here are some of the things he mentioned, I dont think I can get them all.

200 year old home - the last original home on Methena.

  1. He lived in a home that was 200 years old and the last home on Methena in the traditional style. All the other homes on Methena in this style has become ruins and rebuilt in other villages. No one else lived in these styles of homes still.

  2. He and the house is fully self sufficient. He has a thrasher (something to ground wheat or barley?), barley, olive trees, fruit farms, chicken coup, wood working shop, makes his own tsipuro (Greek hard liquor, definitely spelt wrong), he collects his own rain water. He stores bout 50 tons of water. Methena has no natural water and uses 100% rain water.

  3. He restored his ancient home, and is building a new home at the same time. He is self taught through Youtube (a Canadian youtube channel ğŸ˜¼ ).

  4. So many cats, All the village cats come to his home.

  5. Incredible view of the village, valley, and ocean below. Breathtaking spot and he had a great patio to sit and have a beer.

  6. Elias is a man happy with the place he found for himself. He felt as thought it was fate that brought him to find this house and that the house was made for him. He found out afterwards that the previous owner of the house died the very same month that he was born. A new beginning?

  7. He knows everything about the island. The locals, the tradition. Sad about the fact that the island and its culture is dying. He is 40 years old and he is the youngest person living on the island. In some years there will be no locals left.

  8. He has a beautiful wood shop with collectors tools all these Stanly brand planes. I dont know what those are but he was really excited about them.

  9. George Batis - a famous Greek musician was born in his house. This was in the 30s and all the village back then would come gather in his house for concerts. Back then the village has around 300-400 people and was much bigger than the current nine people who lived there.

  10. Elias is an brilliant photographer and showed us a photo book with all the history of the village. He had photos of all the elder locals doing traditional town tasks such as plowing fields with mules, or washing all the village clothes in a watching. Iâ€™ll add one of the photos below.
