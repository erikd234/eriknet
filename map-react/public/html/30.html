#30 - Following more motorcycles

June 18th

Hey,

I am trying to catch up to present time with these posts. But the app I use to post only allowed posts every 12 hours.

So I will do 2 a day (my time zone 10 hours ahead of Vancouver) until I am caught up.

Here we gooooo. Another day biking north!

We woke up in the morning under the cover of the church and walked across the street back to the restaurant of last night.

They told us to come back in the morning for a coffee.

We drank coffee and said goodbye and hit the road.

Today was the first day that we went on the actual official EuroVelo route. Basically it means that it’s marked as a road good for cycling.

More highway sheep

PS - at this point, seeing hers of sheep on the reads is completely normal. I think we have seen 8 or 9 herds in our trip. It’s pretty easy to pass but you need to be careful with sheep dogs.

Continuing on…

Wow wow wow the ride was unreal. It was a coastal highway with very low traffic and stunning views of the mountains and ocean.

Look at that! WOW

Eventually we stopped on a beach when we entered this stunning bay to cook lunch, swim, and nap in the shade.

Gravel trail to the beach.

We left the beach and continued biking.

the beach!!

More pictures of the scenery

We biked for a while just cursing to ourselves about how beautiful it all is.

God damn bulls everywhere

Probably the most scared I have been on this trip was today…

Bulls. SO MANY bulls just sitting on the road. They have massive horns and we were terrified of them charging us.

Fortunately we always found a way around them. Mainly by walking our biked as far as we could away from them. Terrifying.

At around 6 pm we pulled over at the side of the road to look on the map to see if we could find a campsite in the next 10-15 kilometres.

Now right at this exact moment an elderly Greek man pulled out from his farm on his motorcycle, right beside us.

He both said hello and he asked us what we were looking for. We said a good place to camp and just like that he invited us to camp in his friends garden in his village up the road.

We followed him on his motorcycle about 5-10 kms - doing our best to keep up with him.

Following the old man with the motor bike.

We got to his friends garden. He had fresh cold water and gave us fruit. And we had a garden hose to take a shower.

The garden ish place.

We then walked down to the beach and cooked dinner and watched the sunset.

We talked to the man and learned more about his life, but we were tired and went to bed shortly after.

Nights accommodations

A great day with more of the best biking in my life and kind people :)

Cya tomorrow

-Erik
