25Km, Zurich to some bush in the forest…

We left off the last post on August 1st, national swiss day, with a long 170km bike day in the rain, a broken iphone, and the 1 in a 100,000 chance of bumping into Adam in Zurich just after I had left him in the Alps a few days before due to his injury.

I woke up in house of my dear cycling/doctor friends, Lukas and his girlfriend. I enjoyed an amazing sleep in in a nice bed. A comfortable change from the nights in the mountains.

I remember Lukas’s girlfriend making me a coffee before I left for the day.
Lukas the night before had helped me write down detailed instructions for how to get to an iPhone repair store without google maps. I had written them all down in my journal and memorized them. “Go to the lake, 5 bridged up from the lake, to the left there are 6 iphone repair shops”

It was easy to find! Who needs GPS navigation anyway?

The service was great, it was an apple store. They said they cold change the phone with only a slight risk that the phone would be completley wiped.

In those four hours I biked around Zurich without my phone, I found a quiet place at the university of Zurich where I took a lovely nap on some grass. When I woke up from the nap I realized I lost one of my running shoes somewhere in town… It was extra disapointing because i had just bought them new for this trip. Oh well, no more shoes for me.

I only had one shoe left which I decided I would carry with me until I fully left Zurich just incase another miracle happened and I found the other.

I went back to the repair shop to find that they changed the screen out and gave it back to me fully repaired. Good as new… and I still had all my trip photos.

Once it was fixed I slowly started biking out of Zurich to Basel.

Leaving Zurich I biked along the river, the people who live in Zurich frequently swim in the river and float down it, jumping off the bridges. It looked like a very peaceful way to spend a summer day. I would have tried it but I felt a rush to get moving and leave the city.

I continued along the gravel of the river and felt very very tired and low energy. I thought I could make it at least half way to Basel (about 90 km to basel) but about 15 km into biking beside the river, I got tired and found a place to camp, cooked, watched an italian TV show on Netflix and slept.

In Switzerland wild camping is illegal… So I hid in a bush off the cycling path where no one could see me.

My smiling after my phone was fixed.
